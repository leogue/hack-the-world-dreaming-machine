# Var-JEPA — JEPA is a deterministic special case of a coupled latent-variable VAE; make it generative by optimizing one ELBO

**Authors:** Moritz Gögl, Christopher Yau (University of Oxford; Health Data Research UK) **Venue/Year:** Preprint, March 23 2026 (no published venue) **arXiv:** 2603.20111 **Repo:** none linked in the paper

## TL;DR
The paper argues the "JEPA vs. generative modeling" split is "largely rhetorical rather than structural." It reverse-engineers a coupled latent-variable generative model whose variational inference recovers the canonical JEPA pattern (coupled encoders + context-to-target predictor), where the predictor is a **learned conditional prior** `p_θ(s_y | s_x, z)`. From this it derives **Var-JEPA**, trained by maximizing a single ELBO with no ad-hoc anti-collapse regularizers. It instantiates the framework for tabular data as **Var-T-JEPA** (building on T-JEPA) and shows it consistently beats T-JEPA on downstream probing, is competitive with strong raw-feature baselines, and yields per-sample latent uncertainty enabling selective evaluation.

## Problem & motivation
Standard JEPA (`s_x = f_ctx(x)`, `s_y = f_trg(y)`, `ŝ_y = g(s_x, z)`, loss `E‖ŝ_y − sg(s_y)‖²`) has two issues: (1) it is prone to **representational collapse**, requiring heuristic fixes (stop-gradient, EMA target encoder, variance-covariance penalties, SIGReg); (2) it is framed as fundamentally non-generative, separating it from probabilistic latent-variable modeling. The authors ask: *if JEPA's predictive embedding steps were variational posteriors of a coupled VAE, what generative model would produce them?* Answering this both connects predictive and generative SSL and gives a principled route to avoiding collapse plus latent-space uncertainty quantification.

## Method
- **Coupled latent-variable model** over context `x`, target `y`, latents `s_x, s_y` and auxiliary `z` (captures target variability `s_x` can't explain). Joint factorizes as `p(s_x)·p(z)·p_θ(x|s_x)·p_θ(s_y|s_x,z)·p_θ(y|s_y)`; all distributions Gaussian, priors `N(0,I)`.
- **Variational posterior** `q_φ(s_x|x)·q_φ(z|s_x)·q_φ(s_y|s_x,z,y)` (z depends only on `s_x` to prevent target leakage, mirroring the JEPA constraint).
- **Single ELBO** (Eq. 9) decomposes into 5 weighted terms (Eq. 10): context reconstruction `L_rec`, target generation `L_gen`, `KL(q(s_x)‖N(0,I))`, `KL(q(z)‖N(0,I))`, and `KL(q(s_y|s_x,z,y)‖p_θ(s_y|s_x,z))` — the last is regularization toward the **learned conditional prior**, i.e. the JEPA predictor recovered as a generative component. The reconstruction/decoder terms are what prevent collapse without auxiliary costs.
- **LeJEPA/SIGReg link (Sec 3.7):** via "ELBO surgery," the per-sample KL to a fixed prior decomposes into aggregated-posterior mismatch + an information-bottleneck term, so per-sample KL drives aggregated isotropy comparable to SIGReg's explicit `N(0,I)` matching. The `s_y` term instead targets a learned conditional prior, so it correctly does *not* push `s_y` to isotropic Gaussian. Reparameterization trick + single-sample gradient estimator.
- **Var-T-JEPA (tabular):** feature-level masking (context mask `m_ctx`, K target masks), transformer tokenization of mixed numerical/categorical features, six variational components (context encoder, auxiliary encoder, target posterior, latent-space predictor with mask tokens, context + target decoders with feature-specific heads), KL annealing schedules, AdamW. Downstream embeddings use posterior means (deterministic); uncertainty from posterior covariances drives selective prediction.

## Key results
- **Simulation study (Table 1, 10 ablations A–J, 5 runs):** Full ELBO (A) reaches probe accuracy `0.996 ± 0.002` (s_x) / `0.993 ± 0.001` (s_y) with low aggregated `KL = 0.113 ± 0.037` — matching ELBO+SIGReg variants without needing SIGReg. Removing reconstruction (`α_rec = α_gen = 0`, variant G) collapses probe accuracy to near-chance (`0.571`/`0.543`). Removing the `s_x` KL (E) blows aggregated KL to `8.374 ± 1.944`; removing all KL (I) gives `10.465 ± 2.207` (s_x) and `20.276 ± 11.331` (s_y). Adding SIGReg to the no-KL variant (J) partially compensates but the full ELBO remains best.
- **Downstream tabular (Table 2, accuracy; 5 datasets AD/CO/EL/CC/BM + MNIST + SIM; 6 predictor families):** Var-T-JEPA beats deterministic T-JEPA almost everywhere; T-JEPA frequently collapses (e.g. MLP on Covertype `0.408 ± 0.23` vs Var-T-JEPA `0.679`; MLP on MNIST T-JEPA `0.113` vs `0.822`; FT-Trans MNIST T-JEPA `0.317` vs `0.864`). Var-T-JEPA is competitive with strong raw-feature baselines. **Selective evaluation** (discard most-uncertain 10/20/50%) monotonically improves retained-set accuracy (e.g. MLP on Adult `0.852 → 0.865 → 0.883 → 0.921`).
- **Uncertainty alignment (Fig 3):** latent uncertainty correlates with the simulated ambiguity score (Spearman `0.949` on MNIST, `0.640` on SIM) and detects high-ambiguity samples (AUC `0.986` MNIST, `0.805` SIM).
- F1-score table (Table 3) and α / architecture sensitivity studies (Tables 4–5) confirm robustness; deeper encoders / batch size 256 slightly hurt.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
**Modality identified in the paper: tabular data** (the only trained instantiation, Var-T-JEPA, on Adult/Covertype/Electricity/Credit-Card/Bank-Marketing + MNIST-as-tabular + a synthetic set). This makes it the **odd one out** relative to the EB-JEPA reference set (audio/speech/EEG modalities in audio-jepa, laya, s-jepa) — its value here is conceptual, not a drop-in modality recipe.
- **Energy/loss reframing:** Var-JEPA recasts the JEPA energy (latent prediction error) as the conditional-prior KL term of an ELBO, and shows the auxiliary regularizers an EB-JEPA stack adds (variance-covariance, SIGReg/LeJEPA-style isotropy) are *indirect* forms of the distributional control the ELBO does explicitly. Directly relevant to any hackathon track reasoning about why anti-collapse terms are needed and how they relate to a likelihood.
- **SIGReg/LeJEPA bridge (Sec 3.7):** gives a precise "ELBO surgery" account of when per-sample KL ≈ aggregated SIGReg, useful for the regularization-design track.
- **Uncertainty quantification:** posterior-covariance uncertainty enabling selective prediction is a feature most EB-JEPA variants lack; a candidate add-on for any modality.
- **Direct port path (Appendix A.4):** the authors explicitly sketch a vision/video Var-JEPA (ViT context/target encoders, I-JEPA-style predictor as conditional prior, plus a pixel/token decoder for the ELBO) — a concrete recipe if a team wants a generative, uncertainty-aware JEPA in the audio/video/EEG modalities the other references cover.
- **Most relevant track:** an **energy/regularization-theory or generative-extension track** (collapse prevention + uncertainty), rather than a modality-specific (audio/EEG/speech) implementation track.

## Caveats & open threads
- **Tabular-only validation; no vision/video/audio experiments** — the visual extension (App A.4) is described but never run; scaling is left to future work.
- **No public code repo** linked in the paper.
- The downstream pipeline **deviates from the clean generative story** (App D.5): for tabular probing the target posterior conditions on the full feature vector `w` and `z` comes from the learned encoder `q_φ(z|s_x)` rather than the prior `p(z)`; embeddings are posterior means, not samples. So the reported numbers are not a test-time-generative evaluation.
- Concurrent independent work — Huang (2026), "VJEPA: Variational Joint Embedding Predictive Architectures as probabilistic world models" — also probabilizes JEPA; the authors distinguish Var-JEPA as a *coupled* generative model under a *unified* ELBO. Naming clash with Meta's V-JEPA (video) is a citation hazard.
- ELBO needs KL-annealing schedules per latent to avoid posterior collapse; some KL/architecture settings degrade performance, so it is not entirely hyperparameter-free.

# Laya — The first LeJEPA-based EEG foundation model: latent prediction (+ SIGReg, no EMA teacher) beats reconstruction on noisy clinical EEG

**Authors:** Saarang Panchavati, Uddhav Panchavati, Hiroki Nariai, Corey Arnold, William Speier (UCLA / UCSD / UCLA Mattel Children's Hospital) **Venue/Year:** Preprint (NeurIPS-format submission, includes the NeurIPS Paper Checklist), 2026 **arXiv:** 2603.16281 (v2, 7 May 2026) **Repo:** none stated ("Code will be released after the review period")

## TL;DR
Laya adapts **LeJEPA** (JEPA latent prediction regularized by SIGReg, with **no EMA target encoder**) to EEG, using *masked temporal* prediction over a single shared encoder. On a 14-task EEG-Bench evaluation under **frozen linear probing**, Laya-S (trained on only 10% of the corpus) attains the best clinical mean balanced accuracy (0.603 vs 0.578 for LaBraM), is far more robust to injected noise, and produces PCA embeddings that visibly track seizure onset and L/R motor imagery. Controlled MAE ablations with identical architecture/data isolate the **pretraining objective** (latent prediction vs raw/token reconstruction) as the primary driver of the clinical gains.

## Problem & motivation
EEG foundation models (LaBraM, LUNA, CBraMod, REVE) are pretrained on large unlabeled corpora but, per benchmarks like EEG-Bench, "do not consistently outperform simpler baselines"; frozen linear probing "often yields near-chance performance," forcing heavy fine-tuning. The authors hypothesize a root cause: almost all these models use **reconstruction** objectives on raw EEG, and because EEG is "inherently noisy and dominated by non-task-relevant variability," signal-space reconstruction "biases representations toward high-variance artifacts rather than task-relevant neural structure" (citing Littwin et al. 2024, Balestriero & LeCun 2024 on reconstruction producing uninformative features). The proposed fix is to predict in **latent** space (JEPA), which discards unpredictable variation, and to use LeJEPA's SIGReg so collapse prevention does not rely on a finicky momentum teacher.

## Method
Laya keeps two LeJEPA components, the JEPA latent-prediction loss and SIGReg, but departs from vanilla LeJEPA in two ways: (1) context/target are **observed vs masked temporal segments** of the same EEG sequence (masked prediction, like V-JEPA / S-JEPA) rather than two augmented views; (2) **no cross-view invariance loss** — SIGReg is applied purely as a geometric regularizer on the global (CLS) embedding of the masked sequence.

### Architecture (Figure 1, montage-agnostic by design)
- **Patch embedder:** a depthwise 1D conv applied independently per channel, kernel = stride = patch length P (25 samples), mapping raw `x ∈ R^{B×C×T}` to per-channel patch embeddings `x̃ ∈ R^{B×C×N×E}`. Treating channels independently here "avoids imposing inductive biases tied to specific electrode montages."
- **Dynamic channel mixer (from LUNA):** learned query vectors cross-attend across channels at each temporal patch to compress variable-channel EEG into a fixed-size latent. Electrode 3D coordinates are encoded with **fixed Fourier features** and added before attention (spatially aware yet montage-agnostic). Output is flattened + linearly projected to a brain-state sequence `S ∈ R^{B×N×D}`. A **query specialization loss** penalizes off-diagonal entries of `WWᵀ` (W = query–channel affinity) to discourage redundant queries.
- **Encoder:** standard transformer (x-transformers lib), **RoPE** only (no absolute positional embeddings, to avoid overfitting fixed temporal locations under masking). Applied in two passes: full sequence → `Z` + `z_cls` (with StopGrad on Z for the target), and context-only (target positions masked) → `Z_ctx`.
- **Projector (from LeJEPA):** 3-layer MLP with batch norm, expands dim (`D_up ≫ D`) then compresses to `D_proj < D`; applied to Z, z_cls, Z_ctx.
- **Predictor (MAE/V-JEPA-style):** lightweight Transformer encoder at `D_proj`; learnable mask tokens inserted at masked positions; RoPE for relative temporal structure; predicts latent targets `T̂` at masked positions.

### Objective and training step
The combined loss is `L_Laya = L_LeJEPA + γ·L_query` with `γ = 1`, where `L_LeJEPA = L_JEPA + λ·L_SIGReg`. The per-step algorithm (Algorithm 1):
1. sample contiguous temporal mask m; 2. `Z = Enc(S)`; 3. `z_cls = mean(Z)`; 4. `T = Proj(StopGrad(Z))`; 5. `p_cls = Proj(z_cls)`; 6. `Z_ctx = Enc(S, m)`; 7. `P_ctx = Proj(Z_ctx)`; 8. `T̂ = Predictor(P_ctx, m)`; 9. `L_query = QueryLoss(W)`; 10. `L = MSE(T̂, T[m]) + λ·SIGReg(p_cls) + γ·L_query`.
Key point on collapse control: a **single shared encoder, no separate teacher / no EMA**. **StopGrad** only decouples the targets from the predictor gradient path; **SIGReg** is the actual anti-collapse mechanism.

### Masking & pretraining recipe
Block masking of **60%** of the sequence, blocks spanning **500ms–1s** (5–10 patches). Encoder dim 384, depth/heads 12/6; predictor depth/heads 4/4; projector/predictor dim 128; patch 25 samples; 16 channel queries; channel-mixer dim 32. LR 1e-4, weight decay 0.05, warmup (1K steps) + cosine, min LR 1e-6, bf16-mixed, effective sample batch 512 on **two NVIDIA L40S GPUs**, 16-second crops from 120-second segments. **Laya-S** = 10% of data (3K hours) / 10K steps, **SIGReg λ = 0.05**. **Laya-B** = full 30K hours / 20K steps, **λ lowered to 0.02** for stability over longer training.

### Pretraining corpus
After QC, **913,314 samples, 29,109 hours, 20,940 subjects, 17 channel topologies**, mixing TUH EEG, NMT, EEGDash (filtered OpenNeuro aggregation, ~35 datasets listed), Healthy Brain Network, and several MOABB BCI datasets. Resample 250 Hz, band-pass 0.5–100 Hz, notch 50/60 Hz, native montages preserved, robust scaling, 120-s WebDataset shards. Leakage control: EEG-Bench-evaluation datasets excluded; TUH subjects in downstream val/test removed.

## Key results
Evaluation = **EEG-Bench** (14 BCI + clinical tasks), **frozen linear probe**, balanced accuracy, mean ± std over 5 seeds. Baselines: LaBraM, LUNA, CBraMod, REVE (released checkpoints).
- **Clinical mean (Table 2):** Laya-S **0.603** (best) > Laya-B 0.597 > LaBraM 0.578 > REVE 0.547 > LUNA 0.540 > CBraMod 0.511. Standout tasks: mTBI Laya-B **0.815** (vs REVE 0.682), Parkinson's Laya-S **0.708**, Seizure Laya-S **0.786**, Abnormal Laya-S **0.798**, binary Artifact Laya-B **0.737**. Notably Laya-S wins clinical mean with only **10% of the data**.
- **BCI / motor-imagery mean (Table 1):** Laya-S **0.395** highest but margins are small and per-task results are mixed (e.g. RH vs Feet MI Laya-S 0.586 clearly best; 5-Finger MI led by REVE 0.234). All SSL methods are near chance on fine-grained MI.
- **Controlled MAE ablation (Table 3, identical arch/data/config):** Latent prediction (Laya-S clinical 0.603) > Laya-MAE channel-mixed-token prediction (0.567) > Laya-MAE-Decoder raw-signal reconstruction (0.529). The reconstruction decoder trails Laya-S by **7.4 pts** on clinical mean. BCI means are statistically flat (0.390/0.389/0.395). This isolates **objective**, not architecture/data, as the driver.
- **Noise robustness:** with frozen probes and injected noise (Gaussian, 1/f, EMG, channel dropout), Laya retains **91%** of clean accuracy at 10 dB SNR on abnormal detection vs LaBraM dropping to **78%**.
- **SIGReg ablation:** with **λ < 0.02 the model collapses** to a constant embedding (chance-level downstream); removing the projector drops mean accuracy to 0.476. SIGReg is essential.
- **StopGrad ablation (Table 5):** small effect at Laya-S (clinical −4.4 pp) but large at 20K steps (Laya-B clinical 0.597 → 0.466, **−13.1 pp**); StopGrad matters more for longer training.
- **Representation structure (Figure 2, 12):** PCA-to-RGB of patch embeddings shifts distinctly at seizure onset and separates L/R motor states for Laya, whereas LaBraM shows no such structure.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
This is the most direct possible reference for the **flagship EEG-JEPA track** (the hackathon README's flagship is an EEG-JEPA track on TUH/TUAB inspired by LaBraM). Laya is essentially "eb_jepa on EEG": it sits squarely in the **LeJEPA / SIGReg** corner of the design space the eb_jepa recipe spans.
- **Masking vs two-view:** masked *temporal* prediction (single view), like the V-JEPA branch of the recipe, not the two-augmented-view contrastive branch.
- **Anti-collapse:** **SIGReg + StopGrad, no EMA teacher** — the exact LeJEPA/eb_jepa "regularizer instead of momentum target" choice. This paper is empirical evidence that on a noisy real-world modality, SIGReg-regularized latent prediction beats both reconstruction and (implicitly) the EMA-teacher route, and that StopGrad alone is insufficient (SIGReg is what prevents collapse; λ < 0.02 collapses).
- **encoder / predictor / regularizer mapping:** encoder = transformer + LUNA channel mixer (patch embedder → channel mixer → RoPE transformer); predictor = lightweight transformer at the projector dim; regularizer = SIGReg on the CLS embedding + a LUNA query-specialization aux loss. Projector is a LeJEPA-style 3-layer BN MLP with expand-then-compress.
- **24h reuse:** a team on the EEG track could replicate Laya's pipeline cheaply — it trains on **2 L40S GPUs**, and Laya-S reaches SOTA clinical mean on **10% of the data / 10K steps**, so a sub-day pretraining + frozen-linear-probe loop is realistic. The controlled MAE-vs-latent ablation (swap only the prediction target/decoder, hold arch+data fixed) is a clean, self-contained eb_jepa experiment. The LUNA channel mixer + Fourier electrode-coordinate encoding is the reusable trick for handling heterogeneous montages; the block-masking config (60%, 5–10 patches) and recipe are fully specified in Appendix A / Table 4.

## Caveats / open threads
- **Scaling did not help:** Laya-B (10× data, 2× steps) does *not* beat Laya-S on clinical mean (0.597 vs 0.603); the authors blame compute-limited (2-GPU) inability to sweep schedule length, λ, and data mixture, and had to drop λ from 0.05 → 0.02 for stability. Scaling dynamics of latent-prediction EEG models are an open question.
- **BCI/motor-imagery is weak across the board** (all SSL methods near chance on fine-grained MI); quantitative MI parity may reflect task confounds (subject variability, trial-level decoding) rather than information content. Granular BCI may need joint neural-behavior modeling or subject-level adaptation.
- **Eval scope:** only EEG-Bench classification, frozen linear probe; no retrieval/forecasting tasks (e.g. seizure prediction), no AdaBrain-Bench / EEG-FM-Bench. Linear probing may underestimate representations needing nonlinear adaptation.
- **No code yet** ("released after review"); SIGReg implementation details for EEG, exact projector dims (D_up), and the noise-injection protocol are only partially specified in-text (full curves in appendices, figures only).
- **Unverified by us:** all numbers above are quoted from the PDF tables/text, not independently reproduced.

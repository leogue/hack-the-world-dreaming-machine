# DINO-WM — A task-agnostic latent world model built on *frozen* DINOv2 patch features that enables zero-shot, goal-conditioned planning via test-time MPC, no rewards/demos/inverse-models required

**Authors:** Gaoyue Zhou, Hengkai Pan, Yann LeCun, Lerrel Pinto (Courant Institute, NYU; Meta AI) **Venue/Year:** arXiv preprint, v2 (1 Feb 2025); subsequently published at ICLR 2025 **arXiv:** 2411.04983 **Repo:** https://dino-wm.github.io (code/models open-sourced; predictor based on `lucidrains/vit-pytorch`, encoder from `facebookresearch/dinov2`)

## TL;DR
DINO-WM is a visual world model that learns environment dynamics entirely in the **latent space of a frozen, pretrained DINOv2 patch encoder** — never reconstructing pixels for training. A causal ViT predictor takes a short history of patch-feature frames plus actions and predicts the next frame's patch features, trained purely with an L2 *latent-consistency* loss (teacher forcing). Because the model is task-agnostic (no reward, discount, or termination heads) and trained on offline pre-collected trajectories, downstream tasks are solved as **visual goal-reaching**: at test time, encode the goal image to its patch features and run model predictive control (CEM over the latent rollout, cost = MSE-to-goal-features) to optimize an action sequence — fully zero-shot, with no demos, reward model, or inverse model. Across six environments (maze nav, Reacher, Push-T, Wall, deformable rope/granular) it beats DreamerV3, IRIS, and TD-MPC2, especially on contact-rich manipulation (+45% avg success on hardest tasks, +56% LPIPS), and the patch-feature choice (vs CLS/R3M/ResNet global vectors) is the load-bearing ingredient.

## Problem & motivation
World models can either be **online** (interleave model + policy + data collection — accurate only under the current policy, so they must be retrained per task) or **offline** (train once on a fixed trajectory dataset — task-agnostic given coverage, but existing methods lean on strong auxiliary signals: expert demos, keypoints, pretrained inverse models, or dense rewards, all of which sacrifice generality). The central question the paper poses: is there auxiliary information that makes offline world models work *without* compromising their generality? Their answer: a strong pretrained *visual representation*. Modeling dynamics on compact, spatial, object-centric DINOv2 patch features (rather than raw pixels or task-coupled learned latents) is conjectured to give robust, consistent dynamics that relax the need for task-specific data coverage, while sidestepping (i) the compute cost of pixel/diffusion-space prediction and (ii) the task-entanglement of reconstruction- or reward-coupled latents (DreamerV3, IRIS, TD-MPC2). The authors deliberately decouple task-relevant information from latent prediction.

## Method
**Three components** (POMDP framing, all params jointly denoted θ):
- **Observation model (frozen):** the pretrained **DINOv2** encoder maps each 224×224 RGB image (resized to 196×196) to patch embeddings `z_t ∈ R^{N×E}` = `(14×14, 384)`. Frozen during training *and* testing — this is the core design choice and the opposite of eb_jepa's end-to-end-trained encoder. Argument: perception is a general task that benefits from internet-scale pretraining and should not be relearned per environment.
- **Transition (dynamics) model — the only thing primarily trained:** a **ViT with the tokenization layer removed** (so a decoder-only transformer over patch tokens), depth 6, 16 heads, MLP dim 2048, ~19M params. Takes history `z_{t-H:t-1}`, `a_{t-H:t-1}` (context length H) and predicts `z_t`. Uses a **causal attention mask at the frame level**: each patch `z_t^i` attends to all patches of past frames `{z_{t-H:t-1}^i}` — predicting whole frames at once, treating an observation's patches as a unit (contrast with IRIS, which predicts token-by-token within a frame). Ablation A.4.2: without the causal mask the model "cheats" by attending to future frames in training and collapses at test time as history grows (h=3: 0.08 vs 0.92 SR).
- **Action conditioning:** the K-dim action is mapped through an MLP action encoder φ and **concatenated to every patch vector** `z_t^i`; proprioception, when available, is concatenated similarly. Action embedding dim = 10.
- **Decoder (optional, interpretability only):** transposed-conv stack mapping patches back to pixels, trained with a *separate* reconstruction loss, **fully decoupled** from the dynamics model. Ablation A.4.3: backpropagating decoder loss into the predictor *hurts* (0.80 vs 0.92 SR) — disentangling feature learning from reconstruction is better.

**Training objective (latent-consistency, teacher forcing):** slice trajectories into length-(H+1) segments; for each predicted frame
`L_pred = || p_θ(enc_θ(o_{t-H:t}), φ(a_{t-H:t})) − enc_θ(o_{t+1}) ||²`. Entirely in latent space, no pixel reconstruction. **No explicit anti-collapse regularizer is needed** because the L2 target is produced by the *frozen* DINOv2 encoder (a fixed, non-trivial target — the encoder cannot collapse since it is not trained; only the predictor learns). A `frameskip` hyperparameter sets how far ahead the model predicts (nearby frames are too similar to be a meaningful target).

**Planning (zero-shot, test time):** given current obs `o_0` and goal obs `o_g`, encode both with frozen DINOv2 to `ẑ_0`, `z_g`. Planning cost `C = ||ẑ_T − z_g||²` (MSE between final imagined latent and goal latent). Optimize the action sequence with **Cross-Entropy Method** inside **MPC** (receding horizon, replan, execute first k actions): CEM samples N action sequences, rolls each through the latent dynamics model, keeps top-K, refits the Gaussian, repeats. Gradient descent through the differentiable model is also possible but CEM+MPC wins empirically (GD overfits/open-loop: 0.22 vs MPC 0.98 on Maze). Shared HPs: AdamW, predictor lr 5e-5, decoder lr 3e-4, action-enc lr 5e-4, 100 epochs, batch 32; H=3 (maze/reacher/pusht), H=1 (wall, deformables); frameskip 5 (most) or 1 (rope/granular).

## Key results
- **Six offline environments:** Maze (PointMaze, D4RL), Wall (2-room nav through a door), Reach (DMC Reacher, full-arm pose), Push-T (contact-rich T-block pushing), Rope and Granular (deformable XArm, Nvidia Flex). Metrics: success rate (SR) for the first four, Chamfer Distance (CD) for deformables.
- **Main planning table (Table 1):** DINO-WM SR/CD = Maze 0.98, Wall 0.96, Reach 0.92, PushT 0.90, Rope 0.41 CD, Granular 0.26 CD. Beats DreamerV3 (Maze 1.00, Wall 1.00, but Reach 0.64, PushT 0.30, Rope 2.49, Granular 1.05), IRIS, and especially TD-MPC2 (≈0 everywhere — without reward signal it cannot learn good latents). On simple nav DINO-WM ties SOTA; on contact-rich manipulation it dominates (~+45% avg on hardest tasks).
- **Pretrained encoder ablation (Table 2):** DINO *Patch* (theirs) crushes R3M, ImageNet-ResNet, and DINO *CLS* on everything beyond simple mazes (PushT 0.90 vs 0.42/0.20/0.44; Reach 0.92 vs 0.40/0.06/0.60). The takeaway: **spatial patch features beat global single-vector features** — global features lose the spatial detail manipulation needs.
- **Generalization to novel configs (Table 3):** WallRandom (unseen wall/door positions) 0.82, PushObj (unseen block shapes) 0.34, GranularRandom (unseen particle counts) 0.63 CD — best in each; DINO-WM "learns the general concept of walls and doors." PushObj stays hard for everyone.
- **Decoding quality (Table 4, A.7):** even though DINO-WM never trains on pixels, its decoded rollouts have the **lowest LPIPS** on all four hard tasks (PushT 0.007 vs next-best 0.039; ~56% better than prior SOTA) and highest SSIM — open-loop rollouts are "visually indistinguishable from ground truth."
- **Scaling (A.4.1):** PushT SR rises 0.08→0.92 as dataset grows 200→18,500 trajectories — performance scales with offline data.
- **Speed (A.8):** ~0.014 s/step inference vs up to 3 s/step for deformable simulation; CEM planning ~53 s (100 samples × 10 iters).
- **vs generative video models (4.6, A.6):** AVDC (diffusion) makes realistic-looking but physically implausible frames and an action-conditioned AVDC variant diverges over long horizons — generative video is not yet a reliable world model for precise planning.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
DINO-WM is the **frozen-encoder world-model counterpoint** to eb_jepa's design, and a near-perfect map onto the AC-Video-JEPA + planning recipe (`L_world` in the guide: predictor conditioned on `u_t = q_ω(a_t)`, planning by optimizing actions to reach a goal representation `z_g`).

- **Encoder — the key contrast.** eb_jepa trains its encoder `f_θ` **end-to-end** and prevents collapse with an *explicit* VICReg/SIGReg regularizer `R(z)` (the guide is emphatic: "the only reason it does not cheat is the regularizer," and it deliberately avoids stop-grad/EMA tricks). DINO-WM instead **freezes** a pretrained DINOv2 encoder, which is why it needs *no* anti-collapse term at all — a frozen target cannot collapse. This is a clean, high-signal A/B axis for any robotics/world-model track: swap eb_jepa's trained-encoder + VC-regularizer path for a frozen DINOv2 patch encoder + pure latent-prediction loss, on the same Two Rooms / planning stack, and measure planning success. It directly tests how much of eb_jepa's world-model quality comes from learning the encoder vs from the dynamics predictor.
- **Predictor + action conditioning** drop straight into the eb_jepa `g_φ`/`q_ω` slots: a causal ViT over patch tokens with the action MLP-embedded and concatenated to every patch — a reusable recipe for the predictor block, and the frame-level causal mask (Table 6) is a concrete, transferable fix for history-based dynamics models.
- **Planning = Track 7 (learned cost) and Track 9 (stress-test).** DINO-WM's planner is exactly the guide's distance-in-latent-space MPC (cost = `||ẑ_T − z_g||²`), and the paper's own stated limitation — that this crude distance cost and pure action-space planning are weak — *is* Track 7's premise (replace distance cost with a learned cost/value, TD-MPC style). DINO-WM is the strongest baseline to beat there. For **Track 9**, DINO-WM's WallRandom/PushObj/GranularRandom suites are a ready-made factors-of-variation battery, and its frozen-encoder design is itself a hypothesis for "which term breaks first" (a pretrained encoder may be the cheapest way to survive visual perturbation that the trained VC encoder struggles with).
- **Track 8 (hierarchical).** DINO-WM is explicitly single-temporal-resolution and names a hierarchical high-level-planning + low-level-control extension as future work — same gap Track 8 targets; DINO-WM is the flat baseline to layer a coarse predictor on top of.
- **24h-replicable slice.** The cheapest reproduction is: frozen DINOv2 patch features → ~19M-param causal-ViT predictor with concatenated action embeddings → L2 latent loss → CEM-MPC to a goal image, on one environment (PointMaze or Push-T). This reuses eb_jepa's `JEPA.unroll` and planner almost verbatim, with the encoder simply frozen and the regularizer turned off.

## Caveats / open threads
- **Needs offline data with good state-action coverage** (their datasets are 1k–20k random/expert trajectories per env); the authors flag obtaining coverage as hard for complex environments and suggest pairing with exploration.
- **Requires ground-truth actions** in the offline data — limits training on action-free internet video (a gap relative to V-JEPA-style pure-video pretraining).
- **Distance-in-latent cost + action-space planning is crude** (the authors' own limitation), motivating learned costs (Track 7) and a planning hierarchy (Track 8).
- **DINOv2 is frozen and never adapted** — strong on the in-distribution sim renders here, but no test of whether frozen DINOv2 features suffice on real-robot / heavily out-of-distribution visuals, where end-to-end adaptation (eb_jepa style) might matter.
- **CEM beats gradient descent through the differentiable model**, and the authors note adding training/planning regularization "could further improve performance" but leave it unexplored — an open hook the eb_jepa regularizer machinery could fill.
- **No anti-collapse regularizer studied** because the frozen target makes it unnecessary; this means the paper offers no guidance on the VICReg/SIGReg axis that is central to eb_jepa — the comparison has to be built, not read off.

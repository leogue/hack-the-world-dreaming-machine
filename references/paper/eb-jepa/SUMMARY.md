# EB-JEPA — A lightweight, single-GPU, energy-based JEPA library unifying image SSL, video prediction, and action-conditioned world models for planning.

**Authors:** Basile Terver, Randall Balestriero, Megi Dervishi, David Fan, Quentin Garrido, Tushar Nagarajan, Koustuv Sinha, Wancong Zhang, Mike Rabbat, Yann LeCun, Amir Bar (Meta FAIR; INRIA; NYU; LeCun & Bar equal-contribution senior authors) **Venue/Year:** ICLR 2026 Workshop on World Models **arXiv:** 2602.03604 **Repo:** https://github.com/facebookresearch/eb_jepa

## TL;DR
EB-JEPA is an open-source, educational library that frames Joint-Embedding Predictive Architectures (JEPAs) as Energy-Based Models trained with regularization-based collapse prevention (no negatives, no stop-gradient/EMA). It provides three progressively complex, modular, single-GPU (a few hours) examples sharing one energy formulation: (i) Image-JEPA view invariance, (ii) Video-JEPA temporal prediction, (iii) Action-Conditioned Video-JEPA (AC-video-JEPA) world modeling for goal-conditioned planning. Headline numbers: 91.02% linear-probe accuracy on CIFAR-10, coherent autoregressive rollouts on Moving MNIST, and 97% planning success on the Two Rooms navigation task.

## Problem & motivation
Generative/pixel-space world models (Sora-style video gen, interactive simulators) must model all pixels including task-irrelevant detail, which is computationally expensive and can yield uninformative features for perception. JEPAs predict in representation space instead, capturing semantically meaningful features while avoiding generative pitfalls. But JEPAs have a high barrier to entry: existing planning-capable setups rely on particular conditions (e.g., frozen pre-trained encoders) and large-scale infra. EB-JEPA addresses this gap with concise, well-documented, recombinable implementations meant for rapid algorithmic prototyping and teaching before scaling.

## Method
- **Unified energy formulation.** All three settings minimize prediction error in representation space: `L = Lpred(gϕ(z, u), z') + λR(z)`, with `z = fθ(x)` (encoder), `u = qω(a)` (optional action/control encoder), `z'` the target representation, `R` a collapse-preventing regularizer, `λ` the trade-off. General energy `E(x, x', a) = Lpred(gϕ(fθ(x), qω(a)), fθ(x'))`.
  - **(i) Image-JEPA:** two augmented views x, x'; `gϕ = Id`, no conditioning; `L = ||z − z'||² + λR`. Learns view invariance.
  - **(ii) Video-JEPA:** per-frame `zt = fθ(xt−w:t)`; predictor over context of v+1 frames predicts next representation; no conditioning.
  - **(iii) AC-video-JEPA:** action encoder `ut = qω(at−w:t)`; predictor conditioned on actions → latent dynamics model for planning.
- **Regularizers (collapse prevention).** Two families:
  - **VICReg** = variance (hinge on per-dim std toward γ≈1) + covariance (off-diagonal decorrelation); `R_VICReg = αLvar + βLcov`. Two hyperparameters.
  - **SIGReg** (from LeJEPA, Balestriero & LeCun 2025): enforces isotropic Gaussian N(0,I) embeddings via the Epps-Pulley Gaussianity test along P random 1D projections; single hyperparameter λ, linear time/memory, more stable across architectures/hyperparameters.
  - For image/video, the regularizer is computed in a **projected space** via a learned projector `hψ` (bottleneck design best for SIGReg; larger output dim preferred by VICReg).
- **Multistep rollout training (K steps).** Augments single-step loss with k-step recursive rollout losses Lk for k=1..K (L1 = single-step). `z(k)_{t+1} = gϕ(z(k−1)_{t−v:t}, u_{t−v:t})`, `Lpred = Σ_{k=1}^K Lk`. Aligns training with autoregressive inference, reduces exposure bias; optionally TBPTT (detach gradient after each predictor call). Pareto-optimal around K=4 on Moving MNIST.
- **Extra world-model regularizers (AC-video).** Temporal **similarity** loss `Lsim = Σ ||zt − zt+1||²` (smooth trajectories) and **inverse dynamics model (IDM)** loss `LIDM = Σ ||at − MLP(zt, zt+1)||²` (predict action from consecutive reps; critical against spurious correlations in randomized envs). Full objective: `L = Lpred + αLvar + βLcov + δLsim + ωLIDM`.
- **Goal-conditioned planning.** Planning energy accumulated over an imagined rollout to a goal: `Eplan(a0:H; x0, xg) = Σ_{t=1}^H ||fθ(xg) − ẑt||²`, `ẑ_{t+1} = gϕ(ẑ_{t−v:t}, u_{t−v:t})`. Optimized with **MPPI** (population sampling, Boltzmann/exponential weighting over trajectory costs; combined with elite selection in their implementation) or **CEM**. Cumulative (over all timesteps) cost beats final-state-only cost.
- **Building blocks.** Encoders: ResNet-18 / ViT-S / IMPALA / small ResNet. Predictors: ResUNet (spatial), GRU (temporal), Identity (image). Regularizers: VICReg, SIGReg, sim, IDM. Planners: MPPI, CEM.

## Key results
- **CIFAR-10 (Image-JEPA, ResNet-18, 300 ep, linear probe):** SIGReg 91.02% best / 89.22% avg; VICReg 90.12% best / 84.90% avg. A learned projector adds ~3 points over regularizing encoder outputs directly (None: SIGReg 87.75%, VICReg 87.27%). SIGReg best loss hyperparameter λ=10 (90.88%), best projector 2048×128; VICReg best std=1/cov=100, projector 2048×1024. SIGReg is more hyperparameter-stable (single λ); VICReg matches peak but needs more tuning. A ViT-S example in the codebase reaches 87%.
- **Moving MNIST (Video-JEPA, 50 ep):** k-step rollout training markedly improves downstream Average Precision vs single-step; Pareto optimum ~K=4. Maintains coherent autoregressive digit-motion predictions over long horizons.
- **Two Rooms (AC-video-JEPA, random-wall, 3 seeds × 3 ckpts × 20 episodes):** best **97 ± 2%** success with MPPI + cumulative cost; CEM 96 ± 2%; MPPI last-state-only cost 89 ± 2% (cumulative beats final-only by ~8%). The task is non-monotonic (must first move away from the goal).
- **Regularizer ablation (Two Rooms):** removing **IDM (ω=0) collapses to 1%** (spurious correlations); removing variance (α=0) → 47%; covariance (β=0) → 46%; temporal similarity (δ=0) → 61%. Each of var/cov contributes ~50% absolute; sim ~35%.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
This IS the codebase the hackathon runs on; it underpins every track. The recipe participants extend is the unified pipeline: pick an **encoder** (`fθ`: ResNet-18 / ViT-S / IMPALA), a **predictor** (`gϕ`: Identity for image, ResUNet/GRU for video/AC-video), an optional **action encoder** (`qω`), a **collapse-preventing regularizer** (VICReg's var+cov, or SIGReg's single-λ Gaussianity test, in a projected space), plus for world models the **sim + IDM** terms; train with **K-step rollouts**; and for control, plan in latent space toward a goal observation with **MPPI/CEM** under a cumulative-cost energy. The three reference examples (CIFAR-10 91% image SSL, Moving MNIST video prediction, Two Rooms 97% planning) are the starting points each track builds on. The appendix hyperparameter tables (Tables 5–7) and Algorithm 1 (MPPI) give the exact, reproducible default configs. The "Future Directions" section explicitly lists hackathon-shaped extension axes: advancing regularization theory / auto hyperparameter selection, hierarchical (multi-timescale) world models, and learned cost/value functions for planning.

## Caveats / open threads
- Scope is deliberately educational/small-scale (single-GPU, simple datasets, simulated envs), explicitly NOT state-of-the-art; serious eval is meant to move to larger codebases (JEPA-WMs for frozen-encoder planning, stable-pretraining for general SSL).
- Regularizer hyperparameter sensitivity is real (VICReg can collapse, e.g. std=100/cov=100 → 10% on CIFAR; IDM omission collapses planning); why specific regularizer combinations work is theoretically incomplete (flagged as open).
- Single temporal resolution only — hierarchical/multi-timescale prediction is future work.
- Planning uses simple distance-in-latent-space costs; learned cost/value functions are unexplored here.

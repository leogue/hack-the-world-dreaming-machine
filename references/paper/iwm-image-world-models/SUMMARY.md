# IWM (Image World Models) — Generalize the JEPA prediction task beyond masking to global photometric transformations, learn a *reusable* equivariant world model, and use predictor capacity as a knob to interpolate representations between contrastive (invariant) and MIM (equivariant)

**Authors:** Quentin Garrido, Mahmoud Assran, Nicolas Ballas, Adrien Bardes, Laurent Najman, Yann LeCun (FAIR at Meta; Univ Gustave Eiffel CNRS LIGM; INRIA; Courant Institute & Center for Data Science, NYU) **Venue/Year:** arXiv v1, 1 Mar 2024 (cs.CV); published at CVPR 2024 (23 pages, 16 figures) **arXiv:** 2403.00504 **Repo:** code released under FAIR (`facebookresearch`); builds directly on the I-JEPA codebase

## TL;DR
IWM reframes self-supervised learning as **world modeling**: instead of only predicting masked patches (I-JEPA), the JEPA predictor is trained to also predict the latent effect of **global photometric augmentations** (color jitter, grayscale, blur, solarization) given the transformation parameters as an *action*. The paper isolates the recipe for a *capable* (equivariant) world model — it needs (i) **conditioning** the predictor on the transformation/action, (ii) **strong / destructive transformations** (a hard enough prediction task), and (iii) **predictor capacity** (deeper predictor). The headline reframings: (1) the world model (predictor) is normally discarded after SSL pretraining — IWM shows it should be *kept and finetuned*. **Predictor finetuning** (frozen encoder + tuned predictor) matches encoder finetuning at a fraction of the parameters/cost, and end-to-end IWM tops every finetuning protocol (84.4% IN-1k). (2) The predictor's capacity directly controls the **abstraction level** of the encoder's representations: a *weak/invariant* world model behaves like a contrastive method (great linear probe), a *strong/equivariant* one behaves like MIM (lower linear probe, better with bigger heads). IWM thus interpolates the whole spectrum between contrastive and MIM with a single framework.

## Problem & motivation
World models are central in RL (Dreamer, etc.), where they predict the consequence of an *action* and are **leveraged downstream** (planning). In visual SSL the analogous "world model" — the predictor in I-JEPA, or the decoder in MAE — is treated as scaffolding and **thrown away** after pretraining, because typical vision downstream tasks (classification, segmentation) are unrelated to the prediction task. The authors argue this is wasteful and ask two questions: (1) can we learn a *genuinely capable* image world model (one that can actually apply transformations in latent space, i.e. is equivariant) inside the JEPA framework, and (2) can that world model be *reused* for downstream tasks the way RL reuses its model? The unifying picture (Figure 2): if you regard data augmentations as "actions," then BYOL/SimSiam are *unconditional* JEPAs (→ invariance), MAE is a *conditional generative* world model (predicts in pixel space), and I-JEPA / data2vec are *conditional latent* world models limited to the masking action. IWM extends the latent (JEPA) world model to the full augmentation action set.

## Method
**Architecture (JEPA / I-JEPA style).** Encoder `f_θ` = ViT-B/16; target encoder is an EMA copy `f_θ^EMA` (crucial to avoid collapse — same stop-grad/EMA family as I-JEPA, *not* the explicit-regularizer route eb_jepa takes). Predictor `p_φ` = a separate ViT with tunable depth/embedding dim; it *is* the world model. Naming convention `IWM^Z_{X,Y}`: X = predictor depth, Y = predictor embed dim, Z ∈ {Inv, Equi} (whether it learned an invariant or equivariant world model). The two studied extremes are `IWM^Inv_{12,384}` and `IWM^Equi_{18,384}`.

**View generation.**
- **Target y:** original image → horizontal flip + crop + color jitter only. *No destructive augmentations* (no grayscale) on the target, to keep maximum information in the target representation.
- **Source x:** start from `y`, apply *another* color jitter plus **destructive** augmentations (grayscale, blur, solarization — same set as contrastive SSL), then **mask** parts following I-JEPA (mask `M_x` = union of 4 rectangular masks).
- **Action `a_{x→y}`:** the parameters needed to transform x back to y — the color-jitter difference plus binary flags for whether each destructive aug was applied. This is the "action" the world model is conditioned on.

**Forward + loss.** `z_x = f_θ(x)`, `z_y = f_θ^EMA(y)`. The predictor gets the embedded source patches, the action `a_{x→y}`, and mask tokens `m_a` (positions to predict). Objective: `p_φ(z_x, a_{x→y}, m_a) = ẑ_y` should match `z_y`. Loss is squared L2 over the masked positions: `L = Σ_{i∈M_x^C} || p_φ(f_θ(x), a_{x→y}, m_a)_i − f_θ^EMA(y)_i ||_2^2`.

**Conditioning (Table 1).** Two ways to inject the action: *sequence conditioning* (extra tokens, each fed through a unique linear layer to break the transformer's permutation equivariance) and *feature conditioning* (append the action as extra channels on the mask tokens, then a 1×1 conv mixes and maps back to dim). Both give a good world model (MRR 0.82 / 0.79); **no conditioning gives MRR 0.00** (pure invariance). They use feature conditioning (best downstream).

**Evaluating the world model — MRR.** Mean Reciprocal Rank: build a bank of 256 augmented target images, push the clean image through the predictor to predict a transformed target, find the predicted representation's rank among the bank via nearest neighbors, average reciprocal ranks. MRR≈1 = world model can apply the transform (equivariant); MRR≈0 = cannot (invariant/collapsed to invariance).

**Leveraging the world model.** *Predictor finetuning*: freeze the encoder, finetune the predictor as the evaluation head for a discriminative task. The prediction task at finetune time is decoupled from pretraining — simplest/best is to predict an *untransformed full image* using the teacher (null latents, full prediction). *Multitask predictor tuning*: à la prefix/instruction tuning, give the predictor a learned **task token** per task plus a task-specific head/loss, split the batch across tasks — one shared predictor solves many tasks.

## Key results (all ViT-B/16, ImageNet-1k unless noted)
- **World-model recipe (Table 2, MRR).** Jitter-only is too easy (IWM 0.11–0.25); adding destructive augs + deeper predictor is what unlocks equivariance: `(18,384)` with destructive = **0.79**, with strong jitter = **0.85**; I-JEPA (masking only) = **0.00** throughout. Depth matters: 12-layer predictor reaches jitter equivariance 1/5 runs, 18-layer 4/5.
- **Finetuning (Table 4).** Predictor finetuning of the *equivariant* IWM: **83.3%** with a pretrained predictor vs 81.5% random-init (**+1.8** — the world model carries real signal), beating I-JEPA (+0.9) and MAE (+0.3, i.e. MAE's "world model" ≈ random head). End-to-end `IWM^Equi_{18,384}` = **84.4%**, best of all protocols.
- **Efficiency (Fig. 3 / §5.1).** End-to-end ViT-B IWM (84.4%, 121M params) ≈ encoder-finetuned ViT-L (84.3%, 307M) — reusing the world model rivals scaling the encoder. Predictor finetuning beats encoder finetuning by ~1 pt (vs MAE) / ~1.5 pt (vs IWM) at matched parameter budget.
- **Segmentation (ADE20k, Table 6).** `IWM^Equi` predictor finetuning **46.8** and end-to-end **47.0**, beating I-JEPA (45.4) and encoder finetuning — predictor finetuning generalizes beyond classification.
- **Multitask (Table 7).** One predictor finetuned jointly on ImageNet/iNat18/SUN397/Places205 averages **73.5%** vs 73.4% for four separate single-task predictors (+0.1; +2.6 on SUN397) — single shared head, ~zero cost.
- **Probing & the abstraction spectrum (Table 8, Figs. 4–5).** `IWM^Inv_{12,384}`: **74.5** linear / 77.0 attentive — contrastive-like (near MoCoV3's 76.3). `IWM^Equi_{18,384}`: **67.5** linear / 75.1 attentive — MIM-like (near MAE). Correlation of MRR (equivariance) with downstream: ρ = **−0.85** for linear probe, **+0.93** for predictor finetuning, only −0.37 for attentive — equivariance trades linear-probe accuracy for finetuning headroom.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
IWM is a **Core** vision-JEPA paper and the most direct conceptual ancestor of the eb_jepa "predictor-as-world-model conditioned on an action" recipe, applied to the *image* modality rather than video/robotics — it shows the action-conditioned-JEPA idea already pays off when the "action" is just a data augmentation.

- **Core image_jepa track.** IWM = I-JEPA + action conditioning on photometric augmentations. It maps onto eb_jepa's `f_θ`/`g_φ`/`q_ω` slots: the action embedding `q_ω(a)` here is the color-jitter-diff + destructive-aug flags, and the predictor `g_φ` is conditioned on it via feature conditioning (1×1 conv mixing into mask tokens) — a concrete, transferable conditioning recipe for any "predict the effect of a known transformation" objective.
- **Anti-collapse contrast (eb_jepa's central design choice).** IWM prevents collapse with the classic **EMA target encoder + stop-grad**, the very mechanism eb_jepa deliberately *replaces* with an explicit VICReg/SIGReg regularizer. A clean A/B for a regularizer track: re-run IWM-style augmentation prediction but swap EMA/stop-grad for SIGReg, and check whether conditioning-induced equivariance survives without the EMA target. The fact that *un*conditioned IWM collapses to invariance (MRR 0.00) is itself a collapse-mode worth probing with eb_jepa's diagnostics.
- **"Don't discard the predictor" → planning tracks.** IWM's thesis that the trained world model is the reusable asset is exactly the eb_jepa robotics premise (the predictor `g_φ` *is* what you plan with). Predictor finetuning is the image analogue of reusing the dynamics model downstream; the MRR equivariance metric is a cheap, transferable "is my world model actually capable?" probe for any AC-JEPA run.
- **Capacity → abstraction knob.** The result that predictor depth/capacity controls how invariant-vs-equivariant the encoder becomes (ρ=−0.85 linear, +0.93 finetuning) is a directly testable hypothesis on eb_jepa's image stack and a lens for any track that cares whether representations are "semantic/abstract" vs "information-preserving."
- **24h-replicable slice.** I-JEPA ViT-B/16 + add feature conditioning on color-jitter/destructive-aug params + 18-layer predictor → measure MRR and linear-vs-predictor-finetuning on a small subset; reuses eb_jepa's JEPA encoder/predictor scaffolding with a different "action" definition and no MPC needed.

## Caveats / open threads
- **Task–downstream misalignment is explicit.** The world model literally learns to colorize / de-blur / re-jitter images — tasks unrelated to classification — so the predictor *must* be finetuned to be useful (its raw output is "another valid representation," not a class). The benefit is real but only after adaptation; there is no zero-shot transfer of the world model to discriminative tasks.
- **Trade-off, not a free lunch.** Equivariant world models *lose* on linear probing (67.5 vs 74.5) even as they win on finetuning/OOD; "which end of the spectrum" depends on the eval budget you'll actually use. Invariant is better if you only ever linear-probe.
- **Recipe sensitivity.** Equivariance is somewhat stochastic at low capacity (12-layer predictor reaches it 1/5 runs) and requires *strong destructive* augmentations to even appear — weak jitter alone yields invariance. EMA target is reported as "crucial," so removing it (as eb_jepa would want) is non-trivial and untested here.
- **Scope = global photometric (+ mask) transforms only.** No geometric/spatial actions beyond crop/flip, no temporal/video dynamics, no real agent actions — the "world model" is over an augmentation group, not environment dynamics; bridging to true action-conditioned video/robotics world models is left implicit.
- **All ViT-B/16, mostly 300 epochs.** Some baselines (MAE, iBOT, DINO) are quoted at 1600 epochs; ViT-L is only lightly explored (appendix E). Cross-method comparisons span different effective-epoch budgets.

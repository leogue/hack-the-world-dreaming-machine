# LeJEPA — A provably optimal, heuristics-free JEPA objective that drives embeddings toward an isotropic Gaussian via sketched Gaussianity tests (SIGReg).

**Authors:** Randall Balestriero (Brown University; Meta-FAIR), Yann LeCun (NYU; Meta-FAIR) — equal contribution **Venue/Year:** arXiv preprint, 2025 (v3, 14 Nov 2025) **arXiv:** 2511.08544 **Repo:** GitHub repo linked from the paper (URL hyperlinked in the abstract; not in extracted text)

## TL;DR
LeJEPA (Latent-Euclidean JEPA) reduces JEPA training to two principled ingredients: a standard prediction loss between views plus SIGReg (Sketched Isotropic Gaussian Regularization), a new anti-collapse objective. The authors prove the isotropic Gaussian is the unique embedding distribution minimizing worst-case downstream risk (linear and nonlinear probes), then enforce it with SIGReg, which sketches an Epps-Pulley Gaussianity test along many random 1D projections. The result has a single trade-off hyperparameter λ, linear O(N) time/memory, no stop-gradient, no teacher-student, no EMA, no schedulers, and a ~50-line core. Validated across 10+ datasets and 60+ architectures (ResNets, ViTs, ConvNeXts, MaxViTs, Swin) up to 1.8B params; ViT-H/14 reaches 79% linear-probe on ImageNet-1K, and in-domain pretraining beats DINOv2/v3 transfer on specialized domains (Galaxy10, fine-grained sets).

## Problem & motivation
JEPAs predict embeddings of related views but admit collapse (complete or dimensional). State-of-the-art recipes prevent collapse with a stack of under-specified heuristics: stop-gradient, asymmetric views, teacher-student with tuned EMA schedules, whitening/normalization layers, negative samples. These are brittle (sensitive to data/hyperparameters/architecture), often quadratic in batch size or dimension, and lack theory. As a result, JEPA progress has shifted to scaling data/models rather than understanding the objective. LeJEPA asks instead what necessary conditions a JEPA must satisfy, treats them as axioms, and derives a lean objective from first principles.

## Method
- **Axioms.** A good JEPA must (i) solve the prediction task and (ii) make embeddings non-degenerate. The paper proves the right target for (ii) is the isotropic Gaussian.
- **Why isotropic Gaussian (Section 3).** For linear probes, anisotropy provably amplifies both estimator bias (with Tikhonov/ridge regularization, Lemma 1) and variance (Lemma 2), so embeddings should be isotropic. Extending to nonlinear probes (radius k-NN and kernel regression) under a fixed total-variance constraint, the isotropic Gaussian is the *unique* minimizer of the integrated squared bias (Theorem 1). Together this identifies the isotropic Gaussian as minimizing worst-case downstream risk when no task info is known.
- **SIGReg (Section 4).** Distribution matching P_θ = Q is framed as a hypothesis test. To beat the curse of dimensionality, the multivariate test is sketched into M univariate directional tests along random unit directions a ∈ S^{K-1} (Hyperspherical Cramér-Wold, Lemma 3; sufficiency of directional tests, Theorem 2). Each 1D test compares the projected embeddings to a standard Gaussian. The objective averages the per-direction statistics (average, not max, to avoid sparse gradients).
- **Choice of test = Epps-Pulley (Section 4.2).** Three test families compared: moment-based (Jarque-Bera / extended JB) are unstable and non-identifiable with finite moments (gradient norm grows O(k), Theorem 3); CDF-based (Cramér-von Mises, Anderson-Darling, Watson) require sorting → non-differentiable, non-parallel, not DDP-friendly; characteristic-function-based, the **Epps-Pulley** test, compares the empirical characteristic function (ECF) to the target CF in weighted L2. The ECF is a simple mean of complex exponentials, so it is differentiable, all-reduce-friendly for DDP, and has provably bounded loss, gradient, and curvature (Theorem 4). Implemented with a 17-knot trapezoidal quadrature over t ∈ [-5, 5]; minibatch bias is O(1/N) (Theorem 6), negligible even at batch size 16.
- **Beating the curse of dimensionality (Section 4.3).** Two effects keep M small: smoothness (Sobolev regularity of the embedding density makes the directional-error bound decay as |A|^{-2α/(K-1)}, so |A| = O(K) suffices; Theorem 5), and SGD resampling (drawing fresh random directions each step compounds coverage, so |A| as low as 16 resampled beats thousands fixed).
- **LeJEPA loss (Section 5).** `L_LeJEPA = (1−λ)·L_pred + λ·SIGReg`. The prediction loss uses DINO-style global/local views; all views predict the mean of the global-view embeddings (L_pred = (1/V) Σ_v ‖μ_n − z_{n,v}‖² with μ_n the global-view centroid). Core implementation is ~50 lines (Algorithms 1-2), works out of the box on any timm/torchvision backbone with DDP.
- **Relation to prior work.** SIGReg generalizes sliced methods (sliced score matching, sliced Wasserstein). With exact integration, each slice recovers a kernel MMD (but quadratic). Using a degenerate test T = mean² + (std−1)² recovers **VICReg** in the limit (SIGReg enforces E[Z]=0, Cov(Z)=I); the authors argue against this because finite-moment matching permits shortcut solutions.

## Key results
- **Stability.** Linear-probe accuracy is robust across λ (recommend λ=0.05), number of views, batch size (competitive at 128), embedding/projector dims, register tokens, and Epps-Pulley knobs (recommend 17 integration points, [-5,5], ~1024 slices). No catastrophic collapse anywhere in the ablation grids.
- **Architecture-agnostic.** ~50 timm models from 8 families (<20M params) pretrained on ImageNet-10 all reach 91.5–95% top-1 frozen linear probe out of the box; ResNets and ViTs preferred.
- **Heuristics removed.** Predictor and teacher-student dropped without collapse (teacher-student gives only a small ViT boost, mimic-able with SWA); register tokens unnecessary.
- **Scale.** Stable training up to 1.8B-parameter ViT-g; ViT-H/14 reaches 79% linear-probe on ImageNet-1K.
- **In-domain beats frontier transfer.** On Galaxy10 (and fine-grained sets), LeJEPA pretrained directly in-domain beats DINOv2/v3 transfer across data regimes (1-shot to full), both frozen and full-finetune.
- **Few-shot.** Matches/exceeds I-JEPA on 8 datasets while using 100 vs 300 pretraining epochs (~3× cheaper).
- **Loss is a model-selection signal.** LeJEPA's training loss correlates strongly with downstream accuracy (Spearman ~85%, up to ~99% with a simple loss/λ^0.4 scaling law) — enabling label-free cross-validation, which prior JEPA losses do not provide.
- **Emergent semantics.** PCA of last-layer features and [CLS] attention thresholding yield object/background separation and temporally-coherent unsupervised video segmentation.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
SIGReg is one of the two anti-collapse regularizers shipped in `eb_jepa` — it is the **BCS (Batched Characteristic Slicing) loss**, a direct implementation of LeJEPA's Algorithm 1. See `eb_jepa/losses.py` (`epps_pulley`, `class BCS`): random unit-norm projections of shape `(K, num_slices)` resampled per step via a seeded generator, Epps-Pulley statistic with `torch.trapz` quadrature, `all_reduce` for DDP, total `L = invariance_loss + λ·bcs` — matching the paper's SIGReg + L_pred recipe. Configs: `examples/image_jepa/cfgs/sigreg.yaml`, with SIGReg vs VICReg discussed in `examples/image_jepa/README.md`. This paper is the primary reference for the **regularizer choice across all tracks**: every track's encoder/predictor/regularizer recipe can swap VICReg → SIGReg, and LeJEPA's evidence (single λ, architecture-agnostic stability, in-domain pretraining, loss-as-model-selection-signal) directly motivates that choice. Most relevant to the **image_jepa track** (where SIGReg gives EB-JEPA's best CIFAR-10 linear-probe result, 91.02%), and it grounds the EB-JEPA paper's own SIGReg summary line ("SIGReg (from LeJEPA, Balestriero & LeCun 2025)"). The shared encoder → optional predictor → SIGReg-on-projected-embeddings mapping is identical: encoder f_θ, prediction loss between views, SIGReg pushing the (projected) embeddings to N(0, I) with one λ.

## Caveats / open threads
- Theory targets *worst-case* downstream risk with no task info; when downstream tasks are known or skewed, isotropic Gaussian may not be optimal.
- Optimality proofs for nonlinear probes rely on regularity assumptions (small-radius / small-bandwidth expansions for k-NN and kernel regression).
- Epps-Pulley introduces an O(1/N) gradient bias; unbiased variants (U-statistic debiasing, sample splitting) are noted but not explored.
- SIGReg enforces the embedding *marginal*; it does not by itself guarantee the prediction task is solved well — λ still trades the two off (though the loss is reported as well-behaved).
- Evaluation is image-centric (plus brief video segmentation); no language/audio/RL benchmarks, and "GitHub repo" / project-page URLs are hyperlinks not present in the extracted PDF text.
- Recovers VICReg as a degenerate special case, against which the paper explicitly advises due to finite-moment shortcut solutions.

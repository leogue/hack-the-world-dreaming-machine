# SAR-JEPA — Predicting *gradient features* (not pixels) is better: a JEPA for SAR ATR that uses the gradient-by-ratio operator as a speckle-robust target signal

**Authors:** Weijie Li, Wei Yang, Tianpeng Liu, Yuenan Hou, Yuxuan Li, Zhen Liu, Yongxiang Liu, Li Liu (NUDT; Shanghai AI Lab; Nankai VCIP) **Venue/Year:** ISPRS J. Photogramm. Remote Sens., 2024 (DOI 10.1016/j.isprsjprs.2024.09.013) **arXiv:** 2311.15153 **Repo:** https://github.com/waterdisappear/SAR-JEPA

## TL;DR
SAR-JEPA adapts the Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture to Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) Automatic Target Recognition (ATR). Instead of reconstructing raw SAR pixels (corrupted by multiplicative speckle noise) or learning a deep target encoder (which "leads to feature collapse with SAR image noise"), it predicts a **hand-crafted, speckle-robust SAR domain feature** as the JEPA target: a multi-scale **gradient-by-ratio (GR)** representation. The masked-image-modeling (MIM) backbone is **LoMaR** (local masked patches, not whole-image masks) modified with an 8-layer MAE decoder ("LoMaR-SAR"). Pre-trained self-supervised on ~94k–100k unlabeled SAR magnitude images pooled from four open datasets, then few-shot fine-tuned/linear-probed on three ATR datasets (vehicle/MSTAR, ship/FUSAR-Ship, aircraft/SAR-ACD), it beats MAE, FG-MAE (HOG), I-JEPA, LoMaR, and ImageNet supervised init, and keeps improving as unlabeled SAR data grows — arguing toward a SAR foundation model.

## Problem & motivation
SAR ATR is bottlenecked by tiny labeled datasets ("at most ten annotation categories and a few thousand images"), motivating SSL on the growing pool of unlabeled SAR. Two SAR-specific obstacles block off-the-shelf SSL: **(i) small targets in large scenes** (remote-sensing scale problem), and **(ii) multiplicative speckle noise** from coherent imaging. The paper's core claim is that the choice of SSL **target signal** matters most: pixel reconstruction (MAE) is corrupted by speckle; HOG's *differential* gradient "is not a constant false alarm rate operator because of the multiplicative speckle noise" and "leads to virtual dots in the strong-scattering region"; and I-JEPA's learnable deep target encoder collapses under SAR noise. The fix is to inject SAR domain knowledge into the target feature via the **ratio** gradient, which is the statistically correct edge operator for multiplicative noise.

## Method
SAR-JEPA = MIM pretext task (LoMaR-SAR) + a fixed SAR-domain **target encoder** (multi-scale GR), framed as a fusion of MIM and the "physics-guided contrastive architecture" (PGCA) idea.

- **Input/masking (Local Masked Patches):** single-channel SAR magnitude image; randomly sample several **local square windows** at different spatial locations and mask tokens within each (following **LoMaR**, with relative positional encoding + mask tokens at the encoder input — "important for LoMaR to avoid feature collapse"). Local masking is chosen because remote-sensing targets are small; "such relationships usually remain in small areas." Pixel-level RingMo-style secondary sampling "does not work for SAR images because the single pixels ... contain multiplicative noise."
- **Online encoder + predictor:** ViTs from MAE as DNN-encoder and DNN-predictor with learnable relative positional encoding; the encoder extracts deep features f_Deep of masked patches and the predictor predicts the target features of the unseen (masked) tokens.
- **Target encoder (the key contribution):** a *non-learnable* operator mapping the full SAR image to multi-scale gradient features f_SAR via **gradient-by-ratio**. Ratio of average R_i = M1(i)/M2(i) over opposite sides of a pixel (computed with four fixed convolution kernels); horizontal/vertical gradients G_H=log(R1), G_V=log(R3); magnitude G_m=sqrt(G_H^2+G_V^2). **Multi-scale**: kernel sizes r = 5, 9, 13, 17 (window 2r+1), concatenated over channels — "small-scale ... finer extraction of small target contours, while large-scale ... focus on large targets, scene edges, and noise suppression." They use the **linear** kernel variant **GRlin** (M=1) over the Gaussian GRGau because GRGau needs costly per-scale sigma tuning.
- **Loss:** MSE in feature space between f_Deep and f_SAR over masked patches.
- **Decoder design:** an **8-layer ViT decoder (from MAE)** beats LoMaR's linear decoder, "less disturbed by SAR image noise than a linear layer."
- **Augmentation:** MAE augs + a SAR-specific random **ColorJitter contrast** adjustment.
- **Settings:** ViT-B backbone, 4x V100, 200 epochs, batch 300, AdamW, base lr 1e-3, cosine, 20 warmup epochs.

## Key results
All metrics = average accuracy (%); few-shot at 10/20/40-shot, fine-tuning (FT) + linear probing (LP).

- **Pretraining set:** ~94,726 (100%) unlabeled SAR magnitude images from **MSAR + SAR-Ship + SARSim + SAMPLE** (the paper says "approximately 100,000"). Downstream: **MSTAR** (vehicle), **FUSAR-Ship** (ship), **SAR-ACD** (aircraft).
- **vs. baselines (Table 6), MSTAR 40-shot FT:** SAR-JEPA **91.6** vs LoMaR 77.0, MAE 69.5, FG-MAE/HOG 70.0, I-JEPA 44.6, ImageNet 40.7. MSTAR 40-shot **LP**: **81.6** vs MAE 63.7, I-JEPA 44.7.
- **FUSAR-Ship 40-shot FT:** **85.8** (SAR-JEPA) vs LoMaR 82.7, MAE 78.2; **SAR-ACD 40-shot FT:** **75.5** vs MAE 69.5, LoMaR 67.4. SAR-JEPA is best or tied-best on essentially every cell.
- **Target-feature ablation (Table 4):** GRlin >> alternatives. MSTAR 10-shot FT: **GRlin 70.3**, SAR-HOG 64.4, GRGau 62.8, Pixel 55.3, LPF 57.7, **HOG 30.2** (HOG collapses on target-level SAR — "speckle noise can cause the gradient computation ... to have many noise points").
- **MIM backbone ablation (Table 3):** LoMaR-SAR (8-layer decoder) beats both MAE (global mask) and vanilla LoMaR (linear decoder) across all datasets.
- **MIM vs PGCA (Table 5):** each alone < SAR-JEPA; "equal importance and mutual reinforcement of the two modules." MSTAR 40-shot FT: MIM 84.5, PGCA 88.1, **SAR-JEPA 91.6**.
- **Multi-scale (Fig. 4):** multi-scale concat beats any single scale on FT and LP.
- **Scaling (Fig. 7):** **data**: MSTAR LP 10/20/40 avg rises 46.6 -> 70.3 -> 74.8 going 40% -> 70% -> 100% data ("does not reach saturation"). **Model**: ViT-T -> ViT-S -> ViT-B improves, but **ViT-L overfits** (insufficient data). **Epochs**: 200 is the sweet spot; 400/800/1200 overfit ViT-B.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
This is the **remote-sensing / radar-imaging** track informer, and the cleanest case study of a **hand-crafted, domain-physics target feature** in a JEPA — directly relevant to the eb_jepa target/regularizer axis.

- **Target encoder = a fixed feature extractor, not an EMA network.** Unlike I-JEPA (learnable EMA target) or eb_jepa's EMA/stop-grad path, SAR-JEPA's target encoder is a **frozen, non-learnable operator** (multi-scale gradient-by-ratio). This sidesteps representation collapse entirely without an EMA target or a VICReg/SIGReg variance-covariance term — a third anti-collapse strategy worth an explicit A/B in the hackathon: *frozen domain-feature target* vs *EMA deep target* vs *SIGReg/VICReg regularizer*. The paper directly reports that the learnable I-JEPA target collapses on SAR (I-JEPA MSTAR 40-shot FT 44.6 vs SAR-JEPA 91.6).
- **Gradient-feature target is the reusable trick.** The headline "predicting gradient is better" is a concrete recipe: predict a multi-scale, noise-matched gradient representation instead of pixels. For any noisy/non-RGB modality (radar, sonar, ultrasound, depth), choosing a physics-correct target operator (here: ratio gradient for multiplicative noise) may beat both pixels and learned targets.
- **Local masking for small targets.** Local-patch masking (LoMaR) rather than whole-image masking is the spatial-prior analogue for sensor data with small foreground objects in large scenes — transferable to any JEPA where targets are sparse.
- **24h replicable slice:** weights + code are released (waterdisappear/SAR-JEPA). The lightest reproduction: take eb_jepa's masked-JEPA loop, swap the target encoder for a fixed multi-scale GR operator (four fixed conv kernels + ratio + log + magnitude), run on MSTAR/SARSim, and ablate target feature (Pixel vs HOG vs GRlin) — the GRlin >> HOG >> Pixel ordering is a strong, cheap signal to reproduce.

## Caveats & open threads
- **Frozen hand-crafted target ≠ learned representation.** The strong results come partly from baking SAR edge knowledge into a fixed operator; this is domain-specific and does not by itself test whether a *learned* JEPA target can be stabilized on SAR (e.g. with SIGReg/VICReg instead of GR). The "predicting gradient is better" claim is established *relative to pixel/HOG/learnable-deep targets*, not relative to a regularized learnable target.
- **Aircraft is the weak modality.** SAR-ACD lags vehicles/ships; at SAR-ACD 10-shot, FT < LP, indicating "slight overfitting," attributed to streamlined aircraft scattering (few strong points + shadow edges) and scarce aircraft in pretraining.
- **Scale ceiling.** ViT-L and long-epoch runs **overfit** because the ~95k pretraining set is far below ImageNet scale; the authors explicitly flag that pretraining "needs to scale extensively, like ImageNet" and suggest adding more SAR datasets or formula-driven/simulated data. So the "foundation model" claim is aspirational, bounded by data volume.
- **GR target needs the +1e-2 input offset** ("to prevent zero values") and is tuned to magnitude (single-channel) imagery; complex/polarimetric SAR is not directly handled.
- **Tasks limited to classification.** Detection and segmentation are listed as future work; only few-shot recognition is benchmarked here.

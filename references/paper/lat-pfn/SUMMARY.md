# LaT-PFN — A JEPA + Prior-data Fitted Network for zero-shot, in-context time-series forecasting in latent space.

**Authors:** Stijn Verdenius, Andrea Zerio, Roy L. M. Wang (WAIR, Amsterdam) **Venue/Year:** arXiv preprint (under review), 2024 (v2, May 2024) **arXiv:** 2405.10093 **Repo:** https://github.com/StijnVerdenius/Lat-PFN

## TL;DR
LaT-PFN (LatentTimePFN) is a foundational univariate time-series model that does zero-shot forecasting by performing in-context learning *in latent space*. It fuses two frameworks: the Prior-data Fitted Network (PFN), a meta-learning / amortized-Bayesian scheme that approximates the Posterior Predictive Distribution (PPD) from a context of related series, and the energy-based JEPA, which predicts the next latent state and decouples prediction from decoding. Trained exclusively on a novel context-aware synthetic prior over a normalized abstract time axis, it beats ARIMA / FBProphet / ForecastPFN baselines zero-shot and yields a strong embedding space (beats TS2Vec on UCR classification zero-shot). Notably, multi-step "patch-like" tokens emerge in the per-timestep embeddings without explicit training.

## Problem & motivation
Classical and most deep TSF methods cannot forecast zero-shot — they require per-dataset training, limiting generalization. PFNs (e.g. TabPFN, ForecastPFN) enable in-context, amortized-Bayesian inference by training on synthetic priors, but ForecastPFN treats the "context" as just the single target series' own history, which is really regular TSF on simulated data and offers no genuine example context. Meanwhile time series are inherently stochastic, so reconstruction-style latents carry unnecessary detail. The paper's bet: (i) use a *collection of related series* as a true context to approximate the PPD, and (ii) use JEPA to build a prediction-optimized latent space that is not burdened by reconstructive detail, then forecast in that latent space and decode separately.

## Method
Architecture (Fig. 4) with four trained components, optimized largely with one-way independence (stop-gradients):
- **Embedder** Eθ: an 8-layer dilated MobileNet1D (convolutions empirically beat attention over the sequence dim). Produces context embeddings, the held-out history embedding x̄, and a "prompt" z̄ (function of history values + the *full* time axis incl. horizon, via padding/masking to avoid look-ahead leakage). The **target** ȳ is produced by an EMA copy of the embedder + stop-gradient, selecting only future timesteps (standard JEPA anti-collapse).
- **Predictor** PFNθ: learned average-pooling (cross-attn with a learned query) summarizes each context series into a fixed-length series-vector; a PFN transformer then does self-attention over the example dimension and cross-attention from the prompt to the context. A diagonal-only target mask M keeps held-out series independent (faithful to the original PFN). Latent loss = MSE between latent forecast ŷ̄ and target ȳ.
- **Decoder** Dθ: a 3-layer FFN trained on a *stop-gradiented* latent prediction (JEPA-style independent gradients — found critical for stable upstream embeddings). It outputs a categorical distribution over 100 binned output values (cross-entropy, label smoothing 0.01), making no parametric assumption on the target distribution; values are 2-std z-normalized.
- **System Identification head** Hθ: a Sim2Real-inspired regularizer doing multi-target regression from pooled latent embeddings toward the (unit-normalized) simulation parameters that generated the context, further supervising/stabilizing the latent space.
- **Total loss:** L = λ_latent·L_latent + λ_si·L_si + L_decoder, with λ_latent = 3.77e-3, λ_si = 1e-7.

Two data innovations: a **normalized abstract time axis** (time mapped to a fixed interval relative to each series' forecast start, with variable sampling rate) that consolidates frequency patterns, enables any granularity/horizon, and cuts compute; and a **context-aware synthetic prior with triple sampling** (hyperprior → context ranges → sub-context cluster centers μ1,μ2 → per-series point estimates), adapted from ForecastPFN's prior, that produces coherent-but-varied contexts including deliberately non-informative examples. Trained 24h on a single NVIDIA A10G per seed, with warmup schedules for weight decay and EMA decay, and μ-parametrization for stability.

## Key results
- **Forecasting (Table 1):** Best or tied-best MSE and CRRMSE on ECL, ETTh1, ETTh2, Illness, Traffic vs ARIMA, ForecastPFN, FBProphet — all zero-shot. E.g. Illness MSE 0.23 vs ARIMA 0.50; ECL MSE 0.32 vs ARIMA 0.44.
- **Context scaling (Fig. 8):** Performance improves monotonically with context size, and the trend continues *beyond* the training context size — validating the in-context-learning premise.
- **Embedding quality (Table 2):** SVM on frozen LaT-PFN fixed-length summary embeddings reaches 47.9% accuracy across the 128 UCR datasets, zero-shot, beating TS2Vec (44.8%, trained 35 epochs on those datasets).
- **Emergent structure:** t-SNE of summary embeddings separates by dataset; per-timestep embeddings show discrete, regular "patch-like" tokens; nearest-neighbor patches are visually similar local features; DBSCAN reveals a small set of clusters, suggesting an emergent cross-domain time-series "vocabulary" — analogous to ViT tokens, learned without architectural priors.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
This informs a **new-modality / time-series JEPA track** and is a clean reference for the **energy-based JEPA recipe applied outside vision**. Mapping onto the eb_jepa recipe: the EMA target encoder + stop-gradient + latent MSE prediction loss is the exact JEPA anti-collapse machinery already in eb_jepa; LaT-PFN swaps the ViT backbone for a dilated 1D-CNN embedder over the sequence axis and swaps the masked-patch predictor for a PFN cross-attention predictor that conditions on a *context set* of related series (the "z"/conditioning variable becomes the in-context examples). Two transferable ideas for eb_jepa experiments: (1) the **separate, stop-gradiented decoder** trained as a categorical/binned head for probabilistic readout without touching the latent geometry — a probing/decoding pattern directly portable to eb_jepa's downstream heads; (2) the **system-identification auxiliary regression** toward generative-process parameters as an extra latent regularizer/anti-collapse signal, relevant to anyone exploring auxiliary objectives or world-model-style latent supervision. The emergent-tokens analysis is a ready-made "probe the latent space" deliverable.

## Caveats / open threads
- **Univariate only** — no multivariate, hierarchical, discrete, or zero-inflated series yet (cited as the top limitation; would need new synthetic priors).
- Performance hinges on **context quality** ("context curation," explicitly likened to prompt engineering); bad contexts cause variance. RAG/prompt-tuning proposed as future fixes.
- **Sensitive to initialization and normalization choices**; μ-parametrization and warmup schedules were needed for stability.
- All results are **zero-shot on synthetic-only training**, but TSF benchmarking is poorly standardized (arbitrary horizons/processing) — cross-study comparability is weak, by the authors' own admission.
- λ_si = 1e-7 is tiny; the practical contribution of the sysID head vs. its stabilization claim is not separately ablated in the main text.

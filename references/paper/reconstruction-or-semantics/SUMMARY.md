# Reconstruction or Semantics? — a controlled study showing that for action-conditioned latent-diffusion robotic world models, semantic encoder latents (V-JEPA 2.1, Web-DINO, SigLIP 2) beat reconstruction VAE latents on planning/policy, even when VAEs match or win on pixel fidelity

**Authors:** Nilaksh, Saurav Jha, Artem Zholus (equal) , Sarath Chandar (Chandar Research Lab; Mila — Quebec AI Institute; Polytechnique Montreal; Canada CIFAR AI Chair) **Venue/Year:** arXiv preprint, May 2026 **arXiv:** 2605.06388 (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06388) **Project/Repo:** https://hskalin.github.io/semantic-wm/ , https://huggingface.co/Nilaksh404/semantic-wm

## TL;DR
The paper asks which **encoder-defined latent space** makes the best action-conditioned video **latent diffusion model (LDM)** world model for robotics. Holding the DiT transition model, action conditioning, data (BridgeV2), and training fixed, they train one world model per encoder and vary only the latent interface (encoder + optional adapter + decoder). Six encoders are compared across **three evaluation axes**: (1) planning & downstream policy, (2) pixel/visual fidelity, (3) latent representation quality. Headline finding: **visual fidelity alone is insufficient for world-model selection.** Reconstruction encoders (VAE, Cosmos) can match or exceed semantic ones on pixel metrics, but **semantic encoders win on planning, action recoverability, task-success probing, and policy-in-the-loop success — with V-JEPA 2.1 strongest overall on policy.** Conclusion: pick a latent space that preserves action-relevant structure, not the one that reconstructs images best.

## Problem & motivation
Action-conditioned video world models are becoming a practical proxy for real robot control: roll out candidate actions in a learned video model for policy evaluation, planning, and policy improvement. As these models adopt LDM, the latent space becomes a key design choice. The default is a reconstruction-aligned autoencoder (VAE) optimized for pixel fidelity and stable decoding. But a robotic world model needs more than good video: planning and evaluation require predictions that preserve physical, spatial, and task dynamics. Semantic SSL / vision-language encoders expose object layout and task structure more directly, but are hard to use in diffusion (high dimensionality, off-manifold generation). Prior semantic world models (DINO-WM, V-JEPA 2-AC) are not diffusion models (autoregressive feature-prediction / JEPA predictor); RAE-NWM uses DINOv2 but for navigation, not contact-rich manipulation. The effect of semantic latents on action-conditioned LDM for manipulation was therefore open.

## Method
- **Controlled protocol (Fig. 1).** Fix the DiT transition model, action conditioning, dataset, history length, optimizer, and schedule. Vary only the encoder f_phi, an optional frozen adapter alpha_psi, and the decoder path. Train one LDM from scratch per encoder-adapter pair.
- **Latent world model = frozen encoder + optional frozen adapter + action-conditioned DiT + decoder.** Encoder maps each obs to z_t in R^{N x D} (N = h x w patches, D = native channel dim). For high-dim semantic encoders, a frozen **S-VAE adapter** [Zhang et al.] compresses D -> d=96 (KL-regularized, paired with a learned pixel decoder). Only the DiT is trained; encoder/adapter/decoder stay frozen.
- **Transition model.** Action-conditioned **DiT** [Peebles & Xie] trained with **flow matching**; each layer factorizes attention into a spatial block within a frame and a **causal temporal block** across frames. Sample every 2nd frame, condition on **H=2** history frames, predict **8** future frames. No language conditioning during DiT training. For native (no-adapter) semantic encoders, add a lightweight **wide DDT head** [Wang et al.] to address the width bottleneck; for all non-VAE encoders apply a **dimension-dependent noise-schedule shift** (RAE recipe). At inference, autoregressive 1-frame-at-a-time rollout with a 10-frame sliding context. Token count is fixed at N=256 per frame, so semantic width adds little DiT compute.
- **Six encoders, two families.**
  - **Reconstruction-aligned** (frozen, adapter = identity, native pixel decoder): **SD3 VAE** (D=16), **VA-VAE** (D=32), **Cosmos** CI encoder (D=16).
  - **Semantic** (frozen + optional S-VAE adapter to d=96, learned adapter decoder): **V-JEPA 2.1 ViT-L** (D=1024), **Web-DINO ViT-L** (D=1024, adapted from DINOv2), **SigLIP 2 ViT-L** (D=1152). Evaluated both native and as the d96 adapter variant.
- **Data.** **BridgeV2** (~60K WidowX-250 demos, 13 task families, 7-DoF EE actions, language instructions). For success classification, **SOAR** (~30.5K success/failure episodes, 1:2 split).
- **Three evaluation axes.**
  1. **Planning & policy.** **CEM** action recovery error at k=1 (single-step) and k=4 (multi-step). Policy-in-the-loop: roll out **OpenVLA-7B** inside each world model on 20 BridgeV2 test episodes (8 trials each; subset of 10 for OOD); two VLM judges (**InternVL 3.5**, **Qwen 3.6**) give consensus success rate, Borda rank, plus robustness under distractor objects and OOD instructions.
  2. **Pixel fidelity & scene geometry.** FID, SSIM, LPIPS, FVD, temporal-LPIPS, point-track consistency, plus perceptual/geometric scores from WorldArena (image/aesthetic quality, JEPA similarity, subject consistency, depth AbsRel, dynamic degree, flow score).
  3. **Latent representation quality.** Train an **inverse dynamics model (IDM)** on frozen encoder latents (IDM Pearson r, k in {1,4}) and re-apply it to generated WM latents to measure generation-induced degradation; train a **success classifier** on SOAR latent trajectories, again measuring encoder-vs-WM-latent degradation.
- **Diagnostic (Fig. 2).** Project rollouts onto top-2 CCA directions between IDM features and ground-truth actions; semantic spaces show tighter action-aligned geometry (V-JEPA 2.1 rho1=0.94, rho2=0.90, eta=0.71 vs VAE 0.86/0.85/0.59).

## Key results
- **Policy-in-the-loop (DiT-S, Table 1).** V-JEPA 2.1 variants strongest. Consensus VLA SR: **V-JEPA 2.1_96 = 0.362**, **V-JEPA 2.1 = 0.344**, **SigLIP 2_96 = 0.331**, **SigLIP 2 = 0.325**, **Web-DINO_96 = 0.300** vs reconstruction **Cosmos = 0.244, VA-VAE = 0.175, VAE = 0.169**. Best Borda rank = V-JEPA 2.1 (6). ID SR up to 0.625 (SigLIP 2_96) / 0.600 (V-JEPA, Web-DINO) vs 0.375 (VAE); OOD-distractor SR up to 0.588 (SigLIP 2_96) vs 0.250 (VA-VAE).
- **CEM action recovery (DiT-S, Table 1).** Best k=4 = **V-JEPA 2.1 (0.424)**, then Web-DINO (0.474); best k=1 = **SigLIP 2 (0.082)** / V-JEPA 2.1 (0.084). Reconstruction worse: VAE k=4 = 0.612, Cosmos k=4 = 0.661.
- **Latent quality / IDM Pearson r (DiT-S, Table 2).** On encoder latents, **V-JEPA 2.1 best (whole-video r=0.829, k=1=0.781, k=4=0.840 after WM)**, Web-DINO close behind (0.820); reconstruction far lower (VAE 0.507, Cosmos 0.626). Advantage largely persists after WM generation.
- **Success classifier (Table 2).** Encoder accuracy highest for semantic (V-JEPA 2.1 0.905, Web-DINO 0.906, SigLIP 2 0.903) vs VAE 0.835; after WM generation, **SigLIP 2 best (0.823)**; reconstruction degrades more (VAE 0.716).
- **Visual fidelity (DiT-S, Table 3).** Semantic latents stay competitive and often win: **SigLIP 2_96 best SSIM (0.738)**, **V-JEPA 2.1_96 best FVD (5.224)** and best FID (6.302), Web-DINO variants best on JEPA similarity, subject consistency, depth AbsRel, temporal-LPIPS. VAE keeps sharper local detail but lags on global structure / temporal quality.
- **Scaling (DiT-S -> DiT-L).** Bigger DiT mostly helps reconstruction latents on visuals: at **DiT-L, VAE becomes highly competitive** (best FID 5.351, best FVD 3.495, image/aesthetic/JEPA-sim/depth/dynamic-degree). But VAE/Cosmos **still lag on CEM action recovery (larger k=1 error than all semantic), IDM r, and classifier accuracy** — action-centric gaps do not close with scale.
- **Multi-view (Fig. 5).** Fine-tuning on 3-view BridgeV2 episodes improves CEM action prediction but degrades video quality under limited data; **semantic encoders are more robust to this degradation.**
- **Adapter ablation (Fig. 6).** d96 compression eases diffusion/decoding and helps most metrics, but **hurts native-space strengths: CEM action error, OOD robustness, PCK coverage** — adapter trades fine-grained control geometry for diffusion ease.
- **Failure modes differ.** Reconstruction latents **hallucinate task semantics** (coherent but task-incorrect objects, e.g. VAE/Cosmos invent a basket/towel; ignore OOD instructions). Semantic latents **preserve task intent but miss geometry/contact** (e.g. V-JEPA 2.1 under-opens a drawer).
- **Compute.** With fixed N=256 tokens/frame, high-dim semantic width only affects input/output projections; the wide DDT head and adapter pixel decoder keep total params/GFLOPs competitive (Appx. Table 4).

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
This is strong **empirical validation of the latent-JEPA world-model premise** that the hackathon's `ac_video_jepa` / world-model tracks build on: across planning, action recoverability, task-success probing, and closed-loop policy success, **semantic JEPA-style latents (V-JEPA 2.1 strongest on policy, plus Web-DINO/DINOv2 and SigLIP 2) beat reconstruction VAE latents** even when the VAE wins on raw pixel fidelity. It directly substantiates the design choice to do dynamics in a frozen semantic representation space rather than a pixel-reconstruction space, and the IDM/CCA diagnostics (action recoverability, action-aligned trajectory geometry) are reusable evaluation tools for AC-Video-JEPA on DROID / Two Rooms / Push-T.
- **Important framing caveat:** this is a **diffusion** world-model **comparison study**, NOT a JEPA method. The transition model is an action-conditioned DiT trained with flow matching in a decodable latent; the encoders (incl. V-JEPA 2.1) are used as frozen latent interfaces, and "semantic latent" here means a frozen encoder space, not a learned JEPA predictor with EMA target + anti-collapse regularizer. So it is a baseline / motivation reference for the JEPA tracks rather than a method to reuse.
- Practical recipe transfer: spatial-temporal DiT with causal temporal blocks, shallow-wide DDT head, dimension-aware noise shift, diffusion forcing for AR rollout — and the explicit advice to evaluate on control/policy axes, not visual realism alone.

## Caveats / open threads
- **Scoped to BridgeV2** manipulation and a single WidowX embodiment; broader embodiments/domains/data regimes untested.
- Policy-in-the-loop uses a **fixed VLA (OpenVLA-7B) inside generated rollouts**; policy improvement and sim-to-real are complementary uses left to future work.
- Success judgments partly rely on **VLM evaluators** (potential evaluator bias); mitigated by aggregating two VLMs (InternVL 3.5 + Qwen 3.6) and pairing with non-VLM diagnostics (CEM, IDM, latent classifier, geometric metrics).
- **Adapter trade-off** is not a free lunch: d96 compression helps diffusion/visuals but degrades fine-grained action geometry, OOD robustness, and point-tracking.
- **Scaling caveat:** at DiT-L, reconstruction latents recover much of the visual gap (VAE even wins several visual metrics), so the semantic advantage is specifically on action/task-centric axes, not universal.
- All semantic encoders share a common failure where static scene is preserved while manipulation-relevant details hallucinate.

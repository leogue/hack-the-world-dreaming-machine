# JEPA-DNA — Grounding genomic foundation models by JEPA-style continual training: predict the latent embedding of masked DNA spans, on top of existing MLM/NTP backbones

**Authors:** Ariel Larey, Elay Dahan, Amit Bleiweiss, Raizy Kellerman, Guy Leib, Omri Nayshool, Dan Ofer, Tal Zinger, Dan Dominissini, Gideon Rechavi, Nicole Bussola, Simon Lee, Shane O'Connell, Dung Hoang, Marissa Wirth, Alexander W. Charney, Nati Daniel*, Yoli Shavit* (NVIDIA Israel; Sheba Medical Center; Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai) **Venue/Year:** Preprint, 2026 **arXiv:** 2602.17162 (v2, 25 May 2026) **Repo:** https://github.com/NVIDIA-Digital-Bio/JEPA-DNA

## TL;DR
JEPA-DNA is a **model-agnostic continual-pretraining** framework that bolts a JEPA branch onto an already-pretrained Genomic Foundation Model (GFM) and keeps that backbone's native generative loss (MLM or NTP). The JEPA branch masks contiguous DNA **spans**, encodes the masked sequence with a context encoder, encodes the full sequence with an EMA target encoder, and trains a lightweight predictor to match the **global sequence embedding** (CLS / last-token / mean-pool) in latent space via a cosine loss, plus VICReg variance/covariance to stop collapse. Evaluated on 17 genomic benchmark tasks across three backbones (DNABERT-2, NTv3, HyenaDNA) spanning MLM/NTP and CLS/mean/last-token aggregation, it gives consistent linear-probing and zero-shot gains: "+2.6%, +2.19%, and +3.28%" mean AUROC across all tasks for the three backbones respectively, up to "+8.0%, +6.8%, and +11.2%" on individual tasks, and "establishes a new state-of-the-art for GFMs." Ablations show VICReg is load-bearing and that **SGDM (not Adam/AdamW) is essential for stability**.

## Problem & motivation
GFMs (DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Evo) are trained with token-level generative objectives, MLM or NTP, which "prioritize token-level reconstruction over high-level functional context." The authors argue this makes models "over-allocate capacity to high-frequency sequence 'noise' (e.g. repetitive elements or neutral polymorphisms) at the expense of global functional context," so a model can have high syntactic fidelity without internalizing regulatory logic or long-range interactions. Prior non-generative alternatives have costs: MSA methods (GPN-MSA) need expensive alignments; contrastive methods (DNASimCLR) need augmentations or negative mining. JEPA-DNA's pitch is to capture "functional invariants directly from the raw sequence" with a predictive latent objective, no negatives, no alignments, as a corrective "grounding" layer that anchors token-level knowledge to "a semantic world model of genomic function." It is positioned as the first JEPA on the *primary genomic sequence* (vs GeneJEPA, which works on gene-expression vectors; LLM-JEPA, on text).

## Method
**How DNA is tokenized.** Tokenization is inherited from each backbone, deliberately, to test architecture-agnosticism: DNABERT-2 uses **BPE** (512-token context); NTv3 uses **single-nucleotide** tokens (8,192 bp, U-Net downsample/upsample); HyenaDNA uses single-nucleotide tokens with long convolutions (8,192 bp).

**How DNA is masked.** Span-based masking (not the ~15% random independent-token MLM). It samples multiple contiguous target regions and ramps difficulty via a **masking scheduler**: early in training "1-2 contiguous target regions per sequence, covering 10-30%," ending at "1-4 contiguous target regions covering 30-50%." Two-stage masking ("dual-masking"): (1) input tokens at target spans become `[MASK]`, encoded by the context encoder; (2) a **re-masking** step replaces the context-encoder outputs at masked positions with a learnable latent `[MASK]` embedding before the predictor, so the predictor cannot trivially copy. The same mask is fed to both the JEPA loss and the MLM loss.

**How latents are predicted.** Architecture (Fig. 1):
- **Context-encoder (E_theta):** the GFM backbone on the masked input -> `h_ctx`.
- **Target-encoder (Ebar_theta):** an **EMA** copy of E_theta on the full unmasked sequence -> target latents `z` (EMA momentum scheduled 0.996 -> 1.0).
- **Predictor (P_phi):** for token-aggregated backbones (DNABERT-2, HyenaDNA) a **4-layer Transformer** in a reduced latent space (half backbone hidden dim, 3 heads, pre-norm, GELU, frozen sinusoidal positions); for mean-pooled backbones (NTv3) a **3-layer MLP** on the pooled embedding. Kept "lightweight relative to the backbone to prevent a capacity bypass."
- **Aggregator (A):** maps the latent sequence to one global vector via the backbone's native scheme: `[CLS]` (DNABERT-2), mean-pool (NTv3), last `[SEP]` token (HyenaDNA). The JEPA loss operates on this **global** representation.

**Losses.** Total `L = lambda1*L_llm + lambda2*L_jepa + lambda3*L_var + lambda4*L_cov` (Eq. 6). `L_jepa = 1 - cos(zhat_glob, z_glob)` (cosine, weight 1.0). `L_llm` is the native MLM or NTP (weight 0.1; NTP needs an extra causal-encoder forward sharing weights with the context encoder). `L_var`/`L_cov` are **VICReg** variance (hinge, threshold gamma=1, weight 25.0) and covariance (off-diagonal, weight 0.5), computed via a separate deterministic "variance encoder" forward (no dropout/mask) with a deterministic predictor head, weights tied to the main ones.

**Training.** Two-stage continual training starting from the pretrained backbone. Phase 1: encoder frozen, only predictor trained (first 1,000 steps, lr ~1e-5). Phase 2: encoder unfrozen, joint training (peak lr 5e-6 -> cosine to 1e-6 for DNABERT-2/HyenaDNA; lower for NTv3). **SGD with momentum 0.9, weight decay 0.01**, batch 32 x 4 grad-accum (effective 128), up to 100,000 steps, early stopping on GUE Splice-Site val. Strict **data parity**: continual training reuses subsets of each backbone's own pretraining corpus (DNABERT-2: multi-species, 4.76M sequences of 3,200 bp; NTv3: Open Genome 2, random 8,192-bp crops; HyenaDNA: hg38, ~600k 8,192-bp windows) so gains are attributable to the objective, not new data. The **target encoder is used for inference**.

## Key results
- **Linear probing, per-backbone (Table 1):** gains on "6-8 out of 9 tasks for every backbone." DNABERT-2 mean +2.6% AUROC (max +8.0% on Splice Site); NTv3 mean +2.19% (8/9 tasks, max +6.8% on meQTL); HyenaDNA mean +3.28% (7/9, max +11.2% on GUE Promoter, but **-8.6% on GUE TF Binding** -- the one large regression). Consistent wins on promoter, splice-site, meQTL, and causal-eQTL tasks (hypothesized to be the "global-context" tasks JEPA-DNA suits).
- **New SOTA (Table 2):** vs seven leading GFMs, the best JEPA-DNA-augmented model beats the best individual baseline on most tasks, e.g. Promoter 0.937 vs 0.907, TF Binding 0.849 vs 0.837, Splice Site 0.733 vs 0.713, Causal eQTL 0.711 vs 0.697 (one regression: Common-vs-Rare 0.510 vs 0.589).
- **Zero-shot (Table 3, DNABERT-2):** improves 6/8 variant-effect tasks, mean AUROC +4.1%, with BEND Disease Variant +12.06% (0.680 -> 0.762) and LRB Pathogenic OMIM +5.21%; NTv3/HyenaDNA more variable (Table 7).
- **Multi-seed (Table 6, 5 seeds):** gains stable, relative AUROC up to +7.8%, and JEPA-DNA also **lowers variance** on most tasks.
- **Component ablation (Table 4):** **VICReg is essential** -- MLM+JEPA alone is +1.58%, but +VICReg jumps to +4.79%, and +VICReg+Scheduled-Masking to **+6.23%**.
- **Predictor ablation (Table 5):** attention predictor >> MLP; depth peaks at **4 layers** (+6.22%), degrades by 6-8 layers.
- **Optimizer ablation (Table 9):** **SGDM +6.22%; Adam -12.24%, AdamW -4.45%** -- adaptive optimizers destabilize continual JEPA training.
- **Paradigm ablation (Table 10):** latent-space (global-JEPA) alignment +3.86% beats token reconstruction (MLM-only) +2.06%; the hybrid (JEPA global + MLM tokens + scheduled mask) is best at +6.23%.
- **vs contrastive (Table 11):** beats reimplemented DNASimCLR on all tasks, +7.57% mean.
- **Embedding analysis:** on ~37k chr22 windows / 9 functional classes, the **target encoder** kNN-F1 0.903 vs context encoder 0.877, validating use of the target encoder at inference.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
A clean **new-modality (genomics / DNA-sequence)** datapoint for the eb_jepa recipe and a near-perfect template for a "JEPA on 1D sequences" track. The encoder/predictor/regularizer decomposition maps directly onto eb_jepa: encoder = a frozen-then-finetuned sequence backbone; predictor = lightweight Transformer (4 layers) or MLP at reduced dim; regularizer = **VICReg variance/covariance** (the variance term explicitly shown load-bearing, mirroring eb_jepa's anti-collapse stance). Several reusable design choices for a sequence-modality track:
- **Span masking with a difficulty scheduler** (10-30% -> 30-50%, 1-2 -> 1-4 spans), the I-JEPA-in-vision masking protocol ported to 1D, plus a **re-masking** trick (learnable latent `[MASK]` at masked positions) to block predictor shortcuts.
- **Global-embedding JEPA loss** (cosine on CLS/last-token/mean-pool) rather than per-token I-JEPA prediction; Table 10 reports global-JEPA + MLM beats per-token JEPA, a directly testable eb_jepa ablation.
- **EMA target encoder** route (this is the momentum-teacher corner of the design space, contrast with the LeJEPA/SIGReg no-EMA corner used by Laya/LeWM in this same references set), and **use the target encoder at inference**.
- **Continual-training framing:** "grounding" a pretrained generative model with a JEPA branch while keeping a down-weighted (0.1) native loss, a cheap recipe a hackathon team could run (single B300 GPU, ~5 days at full scale, but far shorter at smaller step counts) without pretraining from scratch.
- Hard-won stability lessons transferable to any eb_jepa run: **use SGDM, not Adam/AdamW**, in this continual-JEPA regime; predictor depth ~4; VICReg weights var=25, cov=0.5; cosine (not L2) latent loss.

## Caveats / open threads
- **Continual training only, not from scratch.** The authors flag training a GFM from scratch with the JEPA objective as future work; all claims are about grounding existing backbones.
- **Not uniformly positive:** HyenaDNA loses **-8.6% on GUE TF Binding** and the best augmented model regresses on Common-vs-Rare (0.510 vs 0.589); zero-shot gains for NTv3/HyenaDNA are mixed (several negative entries in Table 7). Gains concentrate on "global-context" regulatory tasks (promoter/splice/meQTL/eQTL); fine-grained local tasks (sQTL, coding pathogenicity) benefit little.
- **Optimizer fragility:** the method only works with SGDM; adaptive optimizers cause large degradation, suggesting a finicky optimization landscape.
- **VICReg + EMA both required:** removing VICReg collapses most of the gain; this is the EMA-teacher + variance-regularizer route, the paper does not test a SIGReg/LeJEPA-style no-EMA variant (it cites LeJEPA only as future work for long-range scaling).
- **Eval scope:** linear probing + cosine-similarity zero-shot on GFMBench-API tasks; no end-to-end fine-tuning comparison, no generation/design tasks, no long-context (megabase) Evo2-scale runs.
- **Unverified by us:** all numbers are quoted from the PDF tables/text, not independently reproduced; code is on GitHub (NVIDIA-Digital-Bio/JEPA-DNA) but not inspected here.

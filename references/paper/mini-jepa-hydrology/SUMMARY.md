# Mini-JEPA — a fleet of small, sensor-specialized JEPA foundation models for agentic hydrologic intelligence

**Authors:** Mashrekur Rahman (Dartmouth Libraries, Dartmouth College). **Venue/Year:** Preprint submitted to Elsevier (May 2026). **arXiv:** 2605.14120 (v1, 13 May 2026, cs.LG). **Repo:** not provided in the paper.

## TL;DR
Instead of one planetary-scale geospatial generalist (Google AlphaEarth), the paper trains a *fleet* of five small (22M-param) JEPA foundation models, each specialized to a single satellite sensor product, then has an LLM router decide which specialist(s) to consult per hydrologic question. The five Mini-JEPAs share an identical ViT-S backbone, identical JEPA recipe, and 64-d output space, differing only in input sensor. Each Mini-JEPA best reconstructs the environmental variable its sensor physically observes (cross-validated R2 up to 0.97), the five embedding manifolds are geometrically distinct in ways that track sensor physics, and the topography-soil and phenology models add information beyond AlphaEarth (∆R2 up to +0.031 for soil moisture/aridity/precipitation). A Claude-based router hits 100% expected-modality selection; dual retrieval (AlphaEarth + routed fleet) beats AlphaEarth-alone on single-sensor questions (Cohen's d = 1.10, p = 0.031) though aggregate gains are modest.

## Problem & motivation
Geospatial foundation models compress multispectral/radar/thermal observations into dense embeddings for downstream retrieval and NL reasoning. The dominant recipe (train one large model on as much data as possible) has two weaknesses: (1) a single embedding space must amortize its dimensions across physically distinct sensors (optical reflectance, microwave backscatter, thermal emission, seasonal phenology), so it can *compromise* on specialized hydrologic signals that live in an underweighted sensor; (2) planetary-scale models are "expensive to train, frequently closed, and slow to extend or audit," inaccessible to groups without heavy compute or costly API access. The paper proposes small sensor-specialized specialists composed by an agent as a complement or substitute.

## Method
**The fleet.** Five Mini-JEPAs, one per Google Earth Engine product, all sharing an identical recipe so any structural difference is attributable to the *sensor*, not training choices:
- S2-Optical (Sentinel-2 SR annual median, 10 bands), S1-SAR (Sentinel-1 GRD VV+VH median, 2 bands), MODIS-Thermal (MODIS LST day+night, 2 bands), S2-Phenology (Sentinel-2 four quarterly composites, 40 bands), Topo-Soil (SRTM elevation/slope/aspect + 3 SoilGrids props, 6 bands).
- **Data:** 9,704 patches (from 10,000 candidate centers) over CONUS, 128×128 px at 30 m GSD (3.84 km footprint), calendar year 2022, same patch centers reused across all five modalities.
- **Architecture/recipe:** ViT-S backbone (12 layers, 6 heads, hidden 384), 64 tokens of 16×16 px, encoder output linearly projected to 64-d (matching AlphaEarth for direct comparison). Training = I-JEPA + a VICReg regularizer to prevent collapse; context encoder sees 60% of tokens, EMA target encoder sees all, a small predictor maps context→target latents at masked positions. 100 epochs, batch 64, lr 1.5e-4. "A few hours per Mini-JEPA on a single NVIDIA RTX 5090." Rationale for JEPA over MAE: latent prediction avoids spending capacity on sensor-specific noise (SAR speckle, MODIS striping) and is cheaper than pixel reconstruction.

**Three-axis per-model characterization** (mirrors the author's prior AlphaEarth studies [6,7]): (1) dimension-level interpretability via Spearman correlations + Random-Forest permutation importance, with spatial-block CV to avoid autocorrelation inflation; (2) manifold geometry via global participation ratio (PR), MLE local intrinsic dimensionality, and local n80 (PCs for 80% local variance at 2,000 probe points); (3) complementarity with AlphaEarth via CCA + RF regressors on AlphaEarth-alone / Mini-JEPA-alone / concatenated feature sets.

**Agentic system.** Each Mini-JEPA gets a compact "reference card" (dimension dictionary, geometric profile, one-sentence physical-signal statement + what it cannot see, per-variable CV R2 table) that fits in the router prompt. A router LLM (Claude Sonnet 4.6 or Opus 4.7) reads the cards and emits a structured tool-call plan choosing which Mini-JEPAs (and optionally AlphaEarth) to query; each retrieval is a kNN search over a per-modality FAISS index of the 9,704 mean-pooled 64-d embeddings. A synthesis LLM composes the final answer with explicit modality provenance.

**LLM-as-Judge eval.** 40 hydrologic questions in four categories (single-modality, multi-modality, SAR-favorable, AE-favorable), each with an expected-modality set. Three conditions: AE-only, Fleet-only, AE+Fleet (full dual-RAG over all six). Two judges (Claude Haiku 4.5 + GPT-OSS-120B) score five rubric items (grounding, scientific accuracy, completeness, coherence, practical utility) on a 1–5 scale; effect sizes as Cohen's d with paired-bootstrap p-values.

## Key results
- **Sensor physics drives skill:** each Mini-JEPA's strongest variable is the one its sensor observes. MODIS-Thermal → mean annual temperature R2 = 0.97; Topo-Soil → elevation R2 = 0.97; S2-Phenology → precipitation R2 = 0.81; S2-Optical → aridity R2 = 0.73; S1-SAR → precipitation R2 = 0.62. NLCD land cover is the worst-predicted variable for every model (categorical label, not cleanly decoded at 30 m by a 22M encoder). Notably the *best* predictor of precipitation across the fleet is S2-Phenology (vegetation response), not the moisture-sensitive SAR model.
- **Distinct manifold geometries:** global PR spans >2× (S1-SAR = 8.9, S2-Optical/Topo-Soil ≈ 11–12, S2-Phenology = 13.9, MODIS-Thermal = 20.2) despite identical architecture. Local picture inverts: MODIS-Thermal near-1-D locally (mean local n80 = 2.0, std 0.0; smooth latitude×elevation gradient), S1-SAR locally most complex (mean local n80 = 6.0, std 1.8; roughness/moisture/biomass/inundation decorrelate locally). Top-5 dominant dims cover only 21% (S2-Phenology) to 29% (S2-Optical, S1-SAR) of probes.
- **Complementarity with AlphaEarth:** joint feature set beats best single source for soil moisture (Topo-Soil joint, ∆R2 = +0.031; AE 0.49 / Topo-Soil 0.52 / joint 0.55), aridity (Topo-Soil, ∆R2 = +0.026), precipitation (S2-Phenology, ∆R2 = +0.021), and soil moisture again (S2-Phenology, ∆R2 = +0.028). Gains small but consistent in sign on hydrologic targets. Temperature/elevation already saturated by AlphaEarth (R2 = 0.93 / 0.94). Positive joint gains concentrate in Topo-Soil and S2-Phenology columns; thermal and broad-optical signals appear already well-represented in AlphaEarth.
- **Agentic system:** router achieves 100% expected-modality hit rate; selection distribution tracks question physics, doesn't collapse to one favorite. Aggregate scores ~4.4–4.5 across the three conditions (LLM-judge saturation near top of scale); mean per-question AE+Fleet over AE-only = +0.021 (11 improve / 10 decline / 7 tie). But on **single-modality** questions AE+Fleet beats AE-only with Cohen's d = 1.10, p = 0.031, n = 9. Other categories not significant (multi d = 0.04; SAR-favorable d = −0.17; AE-favorable d = 0.05). On physics-matched questions, Fleet-only scores comparably to AlphaEarth → substitution case.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
- **Geoscience/hydrology time-series & fields track:** directly a JEPA applied to spatial satellite fields (multi-band 128×128 raster patches) and seasonal/temporal phenology composites — a concrete non-video, non-robotics domain for energy-based / joint-embedding predictive modeling on environmental data.
- **Small-model angle:** strong demonstration that 22M-param ViT-S JEPAs trained for hours on a single consumer GPU (RTX 5090) can match a planetary-scale generalist on physics-matched tasks. Useful precedent for compute-light EB-JEPA experiments and for the "fleet of specialists vs. one generalist" architectural question.
- **Recipe is exactly the EB-JEPA family:** I-JEPA + VICReg anti-collapse regularizer, EMA target encoder, context (60% tokens) → predictor → target latent at masked positions, 64-d output. Maps cleanly onto the eb_jepa training stack and is a clean cross-domain transfer target.
- **Agentic/interpretability layer:** per-model reference cards + LLM router (Claude Sonnet/Opus) + per-modality FAISS retrieval is a reusable pattern for composing multiple JEPA embedding spaces, and the manifold-geometry probes (participation ratio, local intrinsic dimensionality, n80) are ready-made diagnostics for embedding quality / collapse.

## Caveats & open threads
- Corpus limited to CONUS and a single year (2022); no expected cross-climate/biome/year transfer without retraining. Only 9,704 patches.
- LLM-as-Judge scores saturate near 5/5 for strong system LLMs, so the aggregate response-quality comparison is low-resolution; the large effect appears only in the n = 9 single-modality subset. The full 40-question set detects a large effect there but cannot resolve smaller effects elsewhere.
- Fleet-only is slightly *worse* than AE-only under one judge (Haiku d = −0.19) → AlphaEarth's broad-coverage retrievals still add value; AE+Fleet (dual-RAG) is the operationally interesting condition.
- Judge agreement positive but imperfect (Haiku 4.5 d = 0.18 vs GPT-OSS-120B d = 0.06 for AE+Fleet vs AE-only); inherits each judge's environmental-reasoning bias.
- NLCD joint scores fall below AlphaEarth-alone for every Mini-JEPA — attributed to sample-size/regularization noise, not a substantive finding.
- Fleet members chosen along the *sensor-physics* axis only; specialization along hydrologic regime (snow/arid/urban/tropical) or temporal scale (event/seasonal/interannual) is left to future work. No public repo or released checkpoints stated in the paper.

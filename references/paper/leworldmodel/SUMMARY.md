# LeWorldModel (LeWM) — a stable end-to-end JEPA world model from raw pixels with only one effective hyperparameter

**Authors:** Lucas Maes, Quentin Le Lidec, Damien Scieur, Yann LeCun, Randall Balestriero **Venue/Year:** Preprint (arXiv), 2026 (v3, Jun 2026) **arXiv:** 2603.19312 **Repo:** Code linked from the paper website (stated "Code available here"; explicit URL not in PDF text)

## TL;DR
LeWM is the first JEPA that trains **stably end-to-end from pixels** with a **two-term loss**: a next-embedding MSE prediction loss + the **SIGReg** anti-collapse regularizer (from LeJEPA), which pushes latent embeddings toward an isotropic Gaussian. No EMA, no stop-gradient, no frozen pretrained encoder, no auxiliary supervision. It collapses the loss-hyperparameter count from six (PLDM/VICReg-style) to effectively one (the SIGReg weight lambda). A ~15M-param model trains on a single GPU in a few hours and **plans up to 48x faster** than DINO-WM while matching/beating it on most control tasks.

## Problem & motivation
JEPA world models are conceptually simple but fragile: they collapse (encoder maps everything to a constant to trivially satisfy the prediction loss). Existing fixes each carry a cost: I/V-JEPA use EMA-target + stop-gradient (no well-defined objective they minimize); DINO-WM freezes a pretrained DINOv2 encoder (avoids collapse but caps expressivity at the pretraining distribution and is slow to plan, ~200x more tokens); PLDM trains end-to-end but with a **seven-term VICReg-derived loss** (six tunable weights → O(n^6) grid search, training instability). LeWM aims for the union of the good properties: end-to-end, task-agnostic, pixel-based, reconstruction-free, reward-free, with provable anti-collapse and a single tunable hyperparameter.

## Method
- **Architecture.** Encoder = ViT-Tiny (~5M, patch 14, 12 layers, 3 heads, dim 192); observation embedding is the `[CLS]` token + a 1-layer MLP projector **with BatchNorm** (BN is required because the ViT's final LayerNorm otherwise prevents the anti-collapse objective from being optimized). Predictor = transformer (~10M; body says 6 layers/16 heads/10% dropout, appendix calls it a ViT-S) with **AdaLN action conditioning, AdaLN params zero-initialized** so action influence ramps in gradually; causal masking over a short history (history length 3 for PushT/Cube, 1 for TwoRoom); autoregressive next-latent prediction. Total ~15M params.
- **Losses.** `L = L_pred + lambda * SIGReg(Z)`. `L_pred = ||z_hat_{t+1} - z_{t+1}||_2^2` (teacher-forced next-embedding MSE). **SIGReg** projects embeddings onto M random unit directions (Cramer-Wold: matching all 1D marginals ⇔ matching the joint), applies the univariate **Epps-Pulley** normality test statistic per projection against N(0,1), and averages; SIGReg(Z)→0 ⇔ P_Z→N(0,I). Defaults M=1024 projections, lambda=0.1. M and the integration knots barely affect downstream performance, so **lambda is the only effective hyperparameter** → tunable by O(log n) bisection vs PLDM's O(n^6).
- **Training.** Fully offline, reward-free; trajectories of raw pixels + actions from non-optimal behavior policies. Frame-skip 5 (action blocks), batch 128, sub-trajectories of 4 frames, 224x224 input, ~10 epochs per env. All components optimized jointly end-to-end; gradients flow through everything.
- **Planning.** Test-time MPC: encode `o_1`→`z_1` and goal `o_g`→`z_g`; roll the predictor out to horizon H; minimize terminal latent cost `||z_hat_H - z_g||_2^2` over the action sequence with **CEM** (300 samples, 30 iters, top-30 elites, init var 1, horizon 5). World-model weights frozen during planning.

## Key results
- **Planning success** (vs DINO-WM, PLDM, GCBC, GCIQL/GCIVL): LeWM 96% on **Push-T** (PLDM 92, DINO-WM 74, even beats DINO-WM+proprioception 78); **Reacher** 86 (PLDM 78, DINO-WM 79); **OGBench-Cube** 74 (DINO-WM 86 wins, PLDM 65 — DINO-WM's edge attributed to its 3D/visual complexity favoring the foundation encoder); **Two-Room** 87 (PLDM 97, DINO-WM 100 win — LeWM's weakest case: a very low-intrinsic-dim env makes matching a high-dim isotropic-Gaussian prior with SIGReg counterproductive).
- **Speed / cost.** Full planning in ~0.98s vs DINO-WM ~47s (~48x), thanks to ~200x fewer tokens; competitive under fixed-FLOPs budgets (Push-T 90 vs 13, OGB-Cube 74 vs 48).
- **Stability.** Two-term loss converges smoothly/monotonically; PLDM's seven-term loss is noisy and non-monotonic across components.
- **Physical structure.** Linear/MLP probing of physical quantities (agent/block location, block angle) on Push-T: LeWM beats PLDM, competitive with DINO-WM (DINO-WM's edge credited to ~124M-image pretraining). A post-hoc decoder reconstructs scenes from the 192-d `[CLS]` latent (reconstruction never used in training). t-SNE shows spatial structure preserved. **Temporal straightening** of latent trajectories emerges without any explicit term, and exceeds PLDM which has a dedicated smoothness term.
- **Violation-of-expectation.** Surprise (prediction MSE) spikes significantly on physical perturbations (object teleport, p<0.01 across all envs) but not on visual perturbations (color change) → model is more sensitive to physics than appearance.

## Caveats / open threads
- **SIGReg can hurt on low-dimensional environments** (Two-Room): forcing a high-dim isotropic-Gaussian latent when the true intrinsic dimension is tiny yields a less structured space and *worse* planning than PLDM/DINO-WM. Watch this if you port SIGReg to the repo's Two Rooms baseline.
- **Short planning horizons only** (H=5); long-horizon reasoning is unaddressed and motivates hierarchy.
- **Needs offline data with good coverage**; low data diversity weakens SIGReg.
- **Still requires ground-truth action labels**; the authors suggest inverse-dynamics modeling to relax this (the repo's IDM term is exactly such a component — note the guide warns dropping IDM collapses Two Rooms planning 97%→1%).
- **Minor internal inconsistency** in the PDF: body describes the predictor as a 6-layer/16-head transformer (~10M), appendix D calls it ViT-S; encoder patch size is 14 in the body but the decoder section assumes patch 16 — the decoder is visualization-only and trained post-hoc.
- **Author note:** reference [17] (Ponce, Terver, Hebert, Arbel, ICLR 2026, "Dual perspectives on non-contrastive SSL") is by this repo's user; LeWM cites it as the basis for the claim that EMA+stop-gradient do not minimize a well-defined objective — directly relevant to the EB-JEPA energy-based framing.

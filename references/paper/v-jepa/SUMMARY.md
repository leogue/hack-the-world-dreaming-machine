# V-JEPA — Feature prediction (not pixel reconstruction) is a sufficient stand-alone objective for self-supervised video representation learning

**Authors:** Adrien Bardes, Quentin Garrido, Jean Ponce, Xinlei Chen, Michael Rabbat, Yann LeCun, Mahmoud Assran (†), Nicolas Ballas (†) — FAIR at Meta; Inria; ENS/CNRS/PSL; Univ. Gustave Eiffel; Courant Institute/NYU. († joint last author) **Venue/Year:** arXiv preprint v1, 15 Feb 2024 (posted Apr 2024); later TMLR 2024. **arXiv:** 2404.08471 (single version v1) **Repo:** https://github.com/facebookresearch/jepa (code + models; blogpost linked)

## TL;DR
V-JEPA is the video instantiation of the Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture: train a ViT video encoder to predict, **in representation space**, the features of a masked spatio-temporal region `y` from a visible region `x`, conditioned on the positions `∆y` of the masked tokens. It uses **no** pretrained image encoder, **no** text, **no** negatives, **no** pixel reconstruction. Collapse is prevented by the I-JEPA/BYOL recipe — an **EMA target encoder** (`E_θ̄`) + **stop-gradient** + a learnable predictor — but with an **ℓ1 regression loss** (found more stable than ℓ2). Trained on **VideoMix2M** (~2M public videos: HowTo100M + Kinetics-710 + SSv2). The flagship **ViT-H/16** gets **81.9% K400, 72.2% SSv2, 77.9% IN1K** under a *frozen* backbone with attentive probing — beating pixel-prediction video methods (VideoMAE, Hiera, OmniMAE) on nearly every frozen task while seeing far fewer pretraining samples (270M vs 410M–2400M; ~2x wallclock speedup), and beating large image models (DINOv2, OpenCLIP) by +21 pts on motion-heavy SSv2.

## Problem & motivation
Tests the "predictive feature principle" (Rao & Ballard 1999): representations of temporally adjacent stimuli should predict each other. The concrete question: *how far does feature prediction go as a stand-alone unsupervised objective for video, using modern tools (ViT, masked modeling, JEPA, scale)?* Pixel-reconstruction methods (MAE-style) waste capacity modeling unpredictable low-level detail; predicting in **latent space** lets the model discard irrelevant pixels, yielding more versatile, more label-efficient representations at lower pretraining cost. Prior feature-prediction work either froze the target extractor or used contrastive negatives / supervised forecasting to avoid collapse; V-JEPA removes all of those crutches for video.

## Method
- **Architecture (Fig. 2-3):** x-encoder `E_θ` (ViT) processes the masked clip (masked tokens *dropped*, not zeroed); its outputs are concatenated with learnable **mask tokens** carrying positional embeddings of the `y` locations; a **narrow predictor** `P_φ` (12 ViT blocks, embed dim 384, heads matched to backbone) outputs one embedding per mask token. Targets come from an **EMA y-encoder** `E_θ̄`, with masking applied at the **output** of the y-encoder to get contextualized targets (data2vec-style). No `[cls]` token.
- **Objective (Eq. 1):** `minimize_{θ,φ} || P_φ(E_θ(x), ∆y) − sg(E_θ̄(y)) ||₁`. The naive version (no sg, no EMA) collapses to a constant. **ℓ1** is the deliberate change from I-JEPA's ℓ2. *Theory:* the optimal ℓ1 predictor is the conditional **median**, so the encoder gradient minimizes the conditional **median absolute deviation** of the target — forcing the encoder to capture predictable structure; the EMA keeps the predictor near-optimal and thereby prevents collapse.
- **Masking — "multi-block" (Sec 3.2):** spatial blocks are sampled and **repeated across the full temporal extent** (tube-like), to defeat temporal redundancy and make prediction hard. Two mask families unioned: **short-range** (8 blocks, 15%/frame each) and **long-range** (2 blocks, 70%/frame), block aspect ratio in (0.75, 1.5), giving an **average ~90% masking ratio**. Ablation (Table 4): multi-block > causal-multi-block > random-tube[0.9].
- **Tokenizer / inputs:** clip of **16 frames, temporal stride 4** (~3 s); patches are **16×16×2** (2 frames per token). ViT-L/16 & H/16 at res 224 (batch 3072), H/16₃₈₄ at res 384 (batch 2400). **90K iterations** total; AdamW, lr warmed 2e-4 → 6.25e-4 over 12K iters then cosine-decayed to 1e-6.
- **Downstream eval — attentive probing:** frozen backbone, a learnable **cross-attention pooling** (query token + residual + 2-layer MLP + LayerNorm + linear). Because the loss is unnormalized, linear/average pooling is poor: attentive pooling adds **+17.3 (K400)** and **+16.1 (SSv2)** over average pooling (Table 3). Images are handled by duplicating to a 16-frame still clip.

## Key results
- **Feature vs pixel target (Table 1, matched ViT-L/16, 90K iters):** features beat pixels on frozen K400 **73.7 vs 68.6**, IN1K **74.8 vs 73.3**, ~tie on SSv2 (66.2 vs 66.0); fine-tune K400 85.6 vs 85.4.
- **vs pixel-prediction video methods, frozen + attentive (Table 5, ViT-L/Hiera-L):** V-JEPA **K400 80.8, SSv2 69.5, AVA 25.6, IN1K 74.8, Places205 60.3, iNat21 67.8** — best on every task except IN1K (74.8 vs OmniMAE 75.1 trained directly on images), while seeing only **270M** samples in **90K** iters vs VideoMAE 410M/400K, Hiera 770M/1500K, OmniMAE 2400M/1170K. Fine-tune SSv2 75.1 matches Hiera-L.
- **vs SOTA, frozen (Table 6):** flagship **ViT-H/16: K400 82.0, SSv2 71.4, AVA 25.8**; **ViT-H/16₃₈₄: SSv2 72.2, IN1K 77.4** (77.9 with a 2-layer probe). Beats DINOv2 g/14 / OpenCLIP G/14 / I-JEPA on SSv2 by **>+21 pts**; image models still lead on K400 (DINOv2 83.4) and pure image tasks.
- **Label efficiency (Table 7):** at 5% labels, H/16₃₈₄ holds **K400 68.2 / SSv2 54.0**; cutting labels 10x widens V-JEPA's lead over VideoMAEv2/VideoMAE/MVD (e.g. VideoMAEv2 drops to 37.0/28.0).
- **Predictor is grounded (Sec 6, Fig 6):** a frozen-encoder+predictor + trained conditional **diffusion decoder** (fed only the *predicted* masked-region features) produces samples that are spatio-temporally coherent with the visible video and show object permanence — evidence the latent predictions carry real content, not collapse.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
V-JEPA is the **direct upstream reference for the Video-JEPA and AC-Video-JEPA examples** in the guide — the canonical "predict masked spatio-temporal features in latent space" recipe that the hackathon's `JEPA.unroll` generalizes. Note one **load-bearing contrast with eb_jepa**: V-JEPA prevents collapse the *classic* way (EMA target + stop-grad + predictor), whereas the eb_jepa recipe deliberately drops those tricks and relies on an **explicit VICReg/SIGReg regularizer `R(z)`** ("the only reason it does not cheat is the regularizer"). So V-JEPA maps onto the recipe with the regularizer slot replaced by the EMA/sg machinery — a clean A/B axis.
- **Video-JEPA example (Moving MNIST) and Group-B tracks:** V-JEPA's multi-block tube masking, ℓ1 latent loss, narrow-predictor + mask-token design, and attentive-probe eval are all transferable, near-verbatim, to the guide's encoder/predictor (`f_θ`, `g_φ`) and probing contracts.
- **Track 7 (learned cost/value) & Track 8 (hierarchical):** V-JEPA is the strong *flat, single-timescale, frozen-eval* video backbone to build a planner or a coarse high-level predictor on top of; its predictor block is a ready dynamics module if extended with the action-conditioning `u_t = q_ω(a_t)`.
- **Track 9 (factors of variation) & Track 10 (intuitive physics):** V-JEPA's appearance-vs-motion split (strong on motion SSv2, gap to image models on appearance K400/IN1K) is exactly the "which axis does the representation actually capture?" probe; its grounded-predictor diffusion-decoder visualization is a template for Track 10's emergence check.
- **Cheapest 24h slice:** swap the example's loss target to an EMA target encoder + stop-grad + ℓ1, add multi-block tube masking with ~90% ratio and an attentive probe — i.e., reproduce the V-JEPA collapse-prevention path on Moving MNIST / the Video-JEPA example and compare frozen-probe quality against the VICReg/SIGReg default. **Modality: video (also transfers to static images via clip-duplication).**

## Caveats & open threads
- Authors hypothesize their gains on appearance/image tasks are **data-limited**: VideoMix2M lacks the internet-scale visual diversity of the image models' data; they call for larger, more diverse public video datasets.
- **Attentive (non-linear) probing is essential** — the unnormalized loss yields features that are *not* linearly separable, so a plain linear probe badly understates quality (the +16/+17 pt gaps). Any fair comparison must use the same probe for all baselines.
- Collapse prevention is **empirical/heuristic** (EMA + sg + ℓ1); the median-absolute-deviation argument is a motivation, not a guarantee — exactly the gap the eb_jepa explicit-regularizer line targets.
- V-JEPA is **not generative** and has **no action conditioning** here — turning it into a controllable world model (the AC-Video-JEPA setting) is left to follow-ups (V-JEPA 2 etc.; see sibling summaries `vjepa2` / `v-jepa-2.1`).
- All reported numbers are **frozen-backbone or fine-tune classification/detection**; no planning or control results in this paper.

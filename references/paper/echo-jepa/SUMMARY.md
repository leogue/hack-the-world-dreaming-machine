# EchoJEPA — latent prediction (V-JEPA2) beats pixel reconstruction for a noise-dominated ultrasound modality, at foundation scale.

**Authors:** Alif Munim*, Adibvafa Fallahpour*, Teodora Szasz*, Ahmadreza Attarpour*, River Jiang, Brana Sooriyakanthan, Maala Sooriyakanthan, Heather Whitney, Jeremy Slivnick, Barry Rubin, Wendy Tsang†, Bo Wang† (*equal, †co-senior; UHN / Vector Institute / U Toronto / U Chicago / Philips / UCSF / Cohere Labs) **Venue/Year:** Preprint (arXiv, eess.IV), Feb 2026 (formatted in ICML style) **arXiv:** 2602.02603 **Repo:** https://github.com/bowang-lab/EchoJEPA

## TL;DR
EchoJEPA is the first foundation-scale JEPA for echocardiography (cardiac ultrasound video), pretrained on 18M videos / 300K patients. It adapts V-JEPA2 with three domain tweaks and shows that a latent predictive objective (predict EMA-teacher embeddings of masked tubelets, L1 loss, stop-grad) decisively beats pixel reconstruction (VideoMAE) in a strictly compute-matched comparison: better LVEF/RVSP regression and view classification, ~2x sample efficiency, and 86% less degradation under physics-informed acoustic perturbations. The core thesis: in modalities where pixel fidelity is dominated by stochastic nuisance (speckle, attenuation, shadows), reconstruction wastes capacity memorizing noise while latent prediction captures only spatio-temporally coherent anatomy.

## Problem & motivation
Ultrasound video is dominated by stochastic speckle, depth-dependent attenuation, and acoustic shadows, artifacts with no relation to cardiac anatomy and that vary per acquisition. Prior echo foundation models all couple to nuisance signal: supervised multitask (PanEcho) inherits annotation noise; contrastive VLM (EchoPrime, EchoCLIP) aligns to report language rather than anatomy; masked autoencoders (EchoFM, VideoMAE) must faithfully reproduce speckle to minimize reconstruction loss. The authors argue latent prediction against an EMA target is the right objective because the slowly-evolving teacher downweights unpredictable artifacts and reinforces temporally coherent structure (chamber geometry, wall motion).

## Method
- **Backbone / objective.** Builds directly on V-JEPA2. Input video split into spatio-temporal tubelets, partitioned into visible context x and masked target y. Context encoder E_theta encodes visible tubelets; predictor P_phi takes context embeddings + learnable mask token delta_y (positional) and predicts masked-region embeddings; target encoder E-bar_theta (EMA of context encoder) produces targets. Loss is L1: `L = || P_phi(delta_y, E_theta(x)) - sg(E-bar_theta(y)) ||_1` with stop-grad preventing collapse. No pixel decoder. (This is the standard JEPA encoder/predictor/EMA-target recipe with masking and stop-grad as the anti-collapse mechanism, i.e. no VICReg/SIGReg-style variance/covariance regularizer.)
- **Three domain adaptations vs V-JEPA2 defaults.** (1) Temporal resolution 4 fps -> 24 fps (cardiac events unfold in 50-100 ms). (2) Aspect-ratio aug narrowed (0.75,1.35) -> (0.9,1.1) (standardized acquisition geometry). (3) Crop-scale aug (0.3,1.0) -> (0.5,1.0) (fan-shaped sector; small crops drop cardiac structures).
- **Model variants.** EchoJEPA-G: ViT-Giant (1.1B), 18.1M proprietary videos, pretrain 224^2 / 280 epochs then anneal 336^2 / 80 epochs at reduced LR. EchoJEPA-L (public, reproducible): ViT-Large (300M) on MIMIC-IV-Echo (525K videos). EchoMAE-L: compute-matched VideoMAE control (identical arch/data/aug/compute, only objective differs; global batch 1024, 84K updates, AdamW). EchoJEPA-L training: per-GPU batch 128 -> global 1024, LR 1.75e-4 constant after warmup, EMA momentum 0.99925, bfloat16, patch 16 / tubelet 2.
- **Multi-view attentive probing framework (their second contribution).** Frozen backbone; only a lightweight attentive probe is trained. A study has N clips with view labels; per task select one video per relevant view, sample C clips -> L = V*C streams. Each token gets *factorized* learnable view + clip stream embeddings (E_view in R^{VxD}, E_clip in R^{CxD}, only (V+C)*D params vs L*D for full stream embeddings). Self-attention blocks with a key-padding mask ignore tokens from missing views; a learnable query cross-attends to produce a single D-vector -> linear head. **View dropout** (p_miss=0.1) during probe training for robustness to incomplete studies. Probe fixed at depth R=4, 16 heads, MLP ratio 4 across all models.
- **Robustness protocol.** Physics-informed perturbations: depth attenuation (linear intensity ramp with depth, alpha in {0.3,0.5,0.7}) and acoustic shadow (Gaussian intensity reduction, sigma in {0.1,0.2,0.3}W); applied temporally-consistently across frames (static per video) to stay physically plausible.
- **Unified eval protocol.** Frozen backbones for ALL baselines, identical attentive probe, identical LR/WD sweep (eta in {1e-4, 5e-5}, lambda in {0.01,0.1,0.4}), same early-fusion multi-view framework, with standardized wrappers around PanEcho/EchoPrime so representation quality is the only variable.

## Key results
- **Controlled latent-vs-pixel (EchoJEPA-L vs EchoMAE-L, identical everything but objective):** LVEF MAE 5.97 vs 8.15 (-26.7%); view accuracy 85.5 vs 40.4 (+45.1%).
- **LVEF (MAE, frozen + attentive probe):** EchoJEPA-G 4.26 Toronto / 5.44 Chicago / 3.97 Stanford, beating EchoPrime (5.33/6.71/4.87) and PanEcho.
- **Sample efficiency (view classification):** EchoJEPA-G 78.6% at 1% labels and EchoJEPA-L 57.6% at 1%, both above every baseline trained on 100% labels (EchoPrime 42.1%).
- **Robustness:** EchoJEPA-G avg degradation +2.3% under perturbations vs EchoPrime +16.8% (an 86% reduction); stays <4.2 MAE even under severe degradation. (EchoMAE-L's tiny +0.5% is a floor effect off an already-poor 8.52 baseline.)
- **Multi-view RVSP (MAE mmHg):** EchoJEPA-G 4.54 Toronto (17% better than PanEcho).
- **Adult->pediatric transfer (zero-shot):** EchoJEPA-G 4.32 MAE zero-shot, beating all baselines even after their probe fine-tuning; fine-tuned 3.88 (new SOTA). Reconstruction baseline barely improves with fine-tuning (6.79->6.75) vs JEPA (6.31->5.12).
- **Ablations (RVSP, removing one component from EchoJEPA-G, baseline 4.54):** view dropout largest (+18.3% when removed), early fusion vs late-avg (+12.1%), factorized stream embeddings modest (+2.0%).
- **Interpretability:** attention maps show EchoJEPA localizes on valve leaflets / ventricular walls and shifts focus across the cardiac cycle, whereas VideoMAE attends diffusely to borders/Doppler-color intensity.

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
Informs the **video / world-modeling JEPA track** and any **domain-transfer / robustness** track. It is a near-textbook instance of the eb_jepa recipe: encoder E_theta + predictor P_phi + EMA target encoder, **masking (not two-view)** as the pretext, and **stop-grad + EMA as the sole anti-collapse mechanism** (no explicit VICReg variance/covariance or SIGReg term, no projector). So it maps onto the EMA/stop-grad branch of the eb_jepa energy framework rather than the VICReg/SIGReg branch. The directly transferable ideas: (1) the latent-vs-pixel ablation design (compute-matched VideoMAE control) is the clean way to argue an energy/JEPA objective beats reconstruction on a new modality; (2) the **factorized stream (view+clip) embeddings + cross-attention attentive probe with key-padding masking and stream dropout** is a reusable, cheap frozen-backbone evaluation head for multi-stream inputs; (3) the **physics-informed, temporally-consistent perturbation suite** is a ready robustness benchmark for arguing JEPA representations decouple signal from nuisance. The domain-tweak recipe (raise fps, tighten aspect/crop aug to respect acquisition geometry) is a template for adapting V-JEPA2 to any non-natural-video modality.

## Caveats / open threads
- Strongest model (EchoJEPA-G, ViT-G, 18M videos) is trained on proprietary data; only EchoJEPA-L (ViT-L, MIMIC-IV-Echo 525K) is reproducible/public, so the headline scale claims are not externally verifiable.
- Robustness uses *synthetic* physics-informed perturbations, not prospective clinical hard cases (poor acoustic windows); real-world degradation may differ.
- Anti-collapse rests entirely on EMA + stop-grad; no regularizer-based control reported, so collapse margin / sensitivity to EMA momentum is untested here.
- Improvements are mostly on a few cardiac regression/classification tasks (LVEF, RVSP, view ID); fine-grained tasks (segmentation) are future work.
- EchoMAE-L's pixel baseline is weak in absolute terms (8.15 LVEF MAE), so part of the "latent >> pixel" gap may reflect VideoMAE being a poor fit here rather than a universal law; authors flag the floor effect themselves.
- Privacy / memorization of a 1.1B model on 18M patient videos flagged as an open risk.

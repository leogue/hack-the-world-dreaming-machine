# ECG-PTBXL-JEPA — I-JEPA latent feature prediction on 12-lead ECG beats invariance-based and generative SSL on PTB-XL

**Authors:** Kuba Weimann, Tim O. F. Conrad (Zuse Institute Berlin) **Venue/Year:** Computers in Biology and Medicine, 2024 (arXiv v1 dated Oct 2, 2024) **arXiv:** 2410.13867 **Repo:** https://github.com/kweimann/ECG-JEPA

## TL;DR
This work ports I-JEPA (Assran et al., 2023) almost verbatim to 12-lead ECG: a ViT encoder tokenizes the waveform into patches, contiguous blocks are masked, and a predictor regresses the masked patches' latent embeddings (L1 loss) against an EMA-target encoder. Pre-training on a consolidated 10-database, ~1M-record corpus and fine-tuning on PTB-XL yields a macro AUC of **0.945** on the all-statements task, beating the prior SOTA CPC model (0.942) and the masked-autoencoder ST-MEM (0.933 vs JEPA 0.935 on the ST-MEM superclass split). The standout finding is that **JEPA pre-training is always beneficial, even with zero extra data** (ViT-XS pretrained only on PTB-XL's own training partition hits 0.940, beating both random-init and pretrained ResNets), and that **small models suffice** — ViT-S (14.3M) beats ViT-B (85.2M), and adding 9 databases beyond MIMIC-IV-ECG barely moves the needle.

## Problem & motivation
ECG classifiers are bottlenecked by the cost/scarcity of expert labels, so SSL pre-training + transfer is the standard fix. The authors argue the two dominant SSL families both have a modality-mismatch problem on ECG: invariance-based / contrastive methods rely on hand-crafted augmentations that don't transfer cleanly from images to physiological time series (and recent work finds they underperform on downstream ECG), while generative / masked-autoencoder methods waste capacity reconstructing low-level waveform detail in input space. JEPA is pitched as the middle path: no hand-crafted augmentations (compatible pairs come from masking), and prediction happens in latent space so the model can ignore irrelevant low-level structure. The paper frames the current ECG SOTA as CPC (Mehari & Strodthoff, 2022, latent future-prediction) and ST-MEM (Na et al., 2024, spatio-temporal MAE), and aims to beat both.

## Method
- **Architecture:** standard I-JEPA. ViT encoder (with a single **register** token, Darcet et al. 2024) + a narrow predictor transformer at half the encoder's embedding size. Three sizes: ViT-XS (3.6M, dim 192, 8 blocks), ViT-S (14.3M, dim 384, 8 blocks), ViT-B (85.2M, dim 768, 12 blocks). No bias, no dropout, MLP ratio 4 (appendix Table 4).
- **Tokenizer:** 1-D strided convolution producing non-overlapping patches of **size 25 samples** over the 12-lead waveform.
- **Objective:** L1 reconstruction loss in latent space, `L = || P_phi(E_theta(x), z) - sg( EMA_E_theta(y) ) ||_1` (Eq. 1). Online encoder sees the unmasked context x; the EMA target encoder sees the full ECG y; collapse is prevented by stop-grad + EMA (no VICReg/SIGReg term). Mask tokens z carry positional encodings of the target positions.
- **1-D masking (their main domain tweak):** a *dynamic contiguous-block* scheme exploiting the 1-D nature of ECG to avoid the overlap-management headaches of 2-D/3-D I-JEPA/V-JEPA masks while keeping a uniform mask count per batch. They mask **75-85% of patches**, with a minimum block size of 5% to prevent fragmentation.
- **Pre-training:** AdamW, **100,000 steps, batch 2048**, cosine LR 1e-3 -> 1e-6 (10k-step warmup), cosine weight decay 0.01 -> 0.1, EMA momentum 0.998 -> 0.9995. ~16 h for a ViT-S on one A100-80GB.
- **Dataset:** 10 public ECG DBs consolidated to **1,011,849 records / ~10.4M ECG-seconds** (Table 1), dominated by MIMIC-IV-ECG (~80%). Preprocessing: NaN interpolation, resample to 500 Hz, per-database z-norm, clip at +/-5 std, random 10 s crops, baseline-wander removal on CODE-15 and St-Petersburg. Three pretraining regimes: ALL DATASETS / ONLY MIMIC-IV-ECG / ONLY PTB-XL.
- **Fine-tuning / eval:** PTB-XL benchmarks at multiple label granularities; macro AUC, 10 runs, std in parens, early-stop on fold 9, test fold 10. Three protocols: linear eval (frozen + cross-attention pooling head), end-to-end fine-tune (linear head on the register token), and two-stage fine-tune. 2.5 s random crops in training, multi-crop (1.25 s stride) averaged at test.

## Key results
- **PTB-XL all statements (71 labels), macro AUC:** ViT-S JEPA (all data) **0.945** two-stage, beating CPC **0.942** and all baselines (Table 2). ViT-B is *worse* (0.940). Linear eval: ViT-S JEPA **0.940** vs CPC 0.927 vs ResNets ~0.883 — JEPA learns the best off-the-shelf representations.
- **No extra data still wins:** ViT-XS pretrained **only on PTB-XL's training partition** reaches **0.940** (fine-tuned) / 0.931 (linear), beating random-init inception1d (0.925) and pretrained xresnet1d50 (0.929), and matching CPC's frozen 0.927.
- **PTB-XL ST-MEM superclass split** (single-label, custom split, Table 3): ViT-S JEPA **0.935** > ST-MEM 0.933, CPC 0.934; linear eval **0.928** crushes ST-MEM's 0.838.
- **Other PTB-XL hierarchies (appendix Tables 6-10):** JEPA beats the best random-init ResNet on diagnostic statements (0.951 vs 0.937), diagnostic subclasses (0.941 vs 0.930), form (0.911 vs 0.899) and rhythm (0.968 vs 0.957); it loses only on diagnostic *superclasses* (0.928 vs resnet1d_wang 0.930).
- **Scaling notes:** going from MIMIC-only to all-10 DBs is "negligible"; randomly initialized ViTs underperform ResNets and *larger* random ViTs converge worse. **Proficiency at the SSL objective != downstream gain:** best validation checkpoints arrive early (10k steps for linear, 20-30k for fine-tune) even as the loss keeps dropping; linear-eval scores actually decline late in pretraining (Figure 2).

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
Strong fit for the **wearable / biosignal track (Track 3), ECG modality** — and a clean, near-canonical I-JEPA reproduction that maps directly onto the eb_jepa recipe:
- **Encoder/predictor/loss slots:** ViT encoder + narrow predictor + L1 latent loss + EMA target + stop-grad = exactly eb_jepa's EMA/stop-grad JEPA path. The anti-collapse mechanism is purely EMA+stop-grad (no variance/covariance term), so this is a natural A/B to swap in eb_jepa's VICReg/SIGReg regularizer and drop the EMA target.
- **Reusable domain trick:** the **dynamic 1-D contiguous-block masking** (75-85% mask, 5% min block, uniform count/batch) is a directly transferable masking scheme for any 1-D multi-channel sensor JEPA; simpler than the 2-D/3-D mask bookkeeping in I-JEPA/V-JEPA.
- **24 h replicable slice:** the **ONLY-PTB-XL** regime is the cheap, self-contained reproduction — ViT-XS, pretrain on PTB-XL's own ~17k-record training split, then linear-probe / fine-tune on the PTB-XL benchmark, and you already beat ResNet baselines (0.940). No need to assemble the 1M-record corpus to get a publishable result.
- **Contrast with ecg-jepa (2410.08559):** both are 2024 "ECG + JEPA" papers and easy to confuse. **This one** (Weimann & Conrad, CBM 2024, code `kweimann/ECG-JEPA`) is a faithful *I-JEPA* port — block masking, EMA L1 latent loss, ViT, evaluated on the standard **PTB-XL** macro-AUC benchmarks against CPC/ST-MEM. **2410.08559** ("ECG-JEPA", Kim et al.) is a separate ICLR-2025-track effort that introduces a *cross-pattern attention* / lead-masking JEPA tailored to multi-lead structure and reports a broader downstream suite (PTB-XL + CPSC2018 + others). If both PDFs live in this repo, keep them distinct: `ecg-ptbxl-jepa` = Weimann/Conrad I-JEPA-on-PTB-XL; `ecg-jepa` = Kim et al. cross-pattern-attention variant. (Note: ST-MEM, Na et al. 2024, is the MAE baseline this paper beats, not the same as 2410.08559.)

## Caveats & open threads
- **Approximate cross-paper comparison:** the ST-MEM comparison (Table 3) uses ST-MEM's *custom* PTB-XL split, so the head-to-head is "approximate," as the authors note; the 0.935 vs 0.933 margin is within noise (std ~0.002-0.003).
- **Margins are thin:** the headline 0.945 vs 0.942 over CPC is ~0.3 AUC points with std ~0.001-0.003 over 10 runs — real but small; the bigger story is the linear-eval gap and the no-extra-data result.
- **Fine-tune underperforms linear eval:** plain end-to-end fine-tuning of ViT-S (0.936) is *worse* than its frozen linear eval (0.940); only two-stage fine-tuning recovers (0.944), which the authors attribute to overfitting / suboptimal fine-tuning hyperparameters — an open tuning thread.
- **Data diversity barely helps:** adding 9 databases on top of MIMIC-IV-ECG is "negligible," and larger models (ViT-B) regress, suggesting the benchmark is near-saturated and/or the model is data-starved at the high end; scaling claims are limited.
- **No VICReg/SIGReg / no variance-covariance ablation:** collapse is handled only by EMA+stop-grad; the regularizer axis central to eb_jepa is untested here.
- **No dataset redistribution:** only code + preprocessing instructions are released (databases are public but separately licensed), so reproduction requires re-downloading the 10 source DBs.

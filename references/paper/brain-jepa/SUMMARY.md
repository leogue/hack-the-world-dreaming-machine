# Brain-JEPA — A fMRI brain-dynamics foundation model that fixes JEPA for noisy neuro time series with a functional-gradient positional code and a structured spatiotemporal mask

**Authors:** Zijian Dong, Ruilin Li (equal contribution), Yilei Wu, Thuan Tinh Nguyen, Joanna Su Xian Chong, Fang Ji, Nathanael Ren Jie Tong, Christopher Li Hsian Chen, Juan Helen Zhou **Venue/Year:** NeurIPS 2024 (Spotlight) **arXiv:** 2409.19407 **Repo:** https://github.com/Eric-LRL/Brain-JEPA

## TL;DR
Brain-JEPA ports I-JEPA to resting-state fMRI: a ViT observation encoder predicts, in latent space, the EMA-target representations of masked blocks of a parcellated brain-activity time series (450 ROIs x 160 timesteps). It adds two domain-specific pieces that make JEPA work on noisy, low-SNR, spatially-unordered fMRI: (1) **Brain Gradient Positioning**, a learned positional code derived from a diffusion-map embedding of the functional-connectivity matrix (a functional "coordinate system" for ROIs, replacing the meaningless anatomical/sine spatial order); and (2) **Spatiotemporal Masking**, which partitions the non-observation region into Cross-ROI (alpha), Cross-Time (beta), and Double-Cross (gamma) targets so the model must forecast in time, generalize across unseen ROIs, and do both at once. Pretrained on 32k UK Biobank scans, it beats the prior fMRI foundation model (BrainLM, an MAE) and CNN/GNN/transformer baselines on 8 tasks across 5 cohorts, with a much smaller fine-tuning-to-linear-probing gap and zero-shot transfer to an Asian cohort despite Caucasian-only pretraining.

## Problem & motivation
fMRI BOLD is an indirect, low-SNR proxy for neural activity, with spatiotemporally sparse signal and no clear structural boundaries (unlike the edges/colors of natural images). The prior fMRI foundation model, BrainLM, is a masked autoencoder (MAE) that reconstructs the raw masked time series; the authors argue this is the wrong objective for such noisy data because (i) forcing pixel-perfect reconstruction conflates noise and signal, (ii) MAE-style generative pretraining is known to give weak off-the-shelf (linear-probe) representations, requiring expensive end-to-end fine-tuning plus an MLP head. Latent prediction (I-JEPA) sidesteps this: the target encoder is free to discard unpredictable artifacts, yielding higher-SNR, more abstract representations. But naive I-JEPA does not transfer to fMRI for two reasons the paper sets out to fix: **(1) no natural spatial order** — ROIs across the 3D brain volume have no inherent sequence, and anatomical labels miss *functional* parcellation (spatially adjacent ROIs can behave very differently); **(2) random multi-block masking is too weak an inductive bias** for the smaller, sparser fMRI datasets, which need stronger structure than ImageNet-scale random masking provides.

## Method
**Backbone / JEPA loop.** Standard I-JEPA structure: a ViT observation (context) encoder f_theta, an EMA target encoder (same arch, weights = linear-schedule EMA of f_theta, stop-gradient), and a narrow ViT predictor g_phi. The predictor takes the observation tokens plus positional embeddings of the target locations and predicts the target-block embeddings; loss is the mean L2 distance between predicted and EMA-target embeddings (averaged over the 3 target regions, K blocks each). No [cls] token; downstream uses average-pooled target-encoder output. Encoders tried at ViT-S/B/L (22M/86M/307M); predictor depth 6 (S/B) or 12 (L) at narrow width (192/384). FlashAttention used.

**Input.** fMRI parcellated into n=450 ROIs (Schaefer-400 cortical + Tian-S3 subcortical), robust-scaled per ROI; default input 450 ROIs x 160 timesteps; patch size p=16 timepoints (10 timestep-patches per ROI). ROIs are shuffled before patchifying.

**Brain Gradient Positioning (spatial PE).** Build a non-negative affinity matrix A from cosine similarity of ROI functional-connectivity profiles (Eq. 1), run a diffusion map (alpha=0.5) to get a gradient matrix G in R^{n x m} (m=30 gradient components by default) — a continuous functional coordinate system where ROI distance encodes connectivity similarity. G is passed through a trainable linear layer to dimension d/2 and concatenated with a standard sine/cosine temporal positional code (also d/2): P = [T, G_hat] in R^{n x d}. The top-3 gradients recover known networks (somatomotor / DMN / visual). Gradients are computed group-level on the 80% UKB pretraining split.

**Spatiotemporal Masking.** With the single observation block excluded, the rest of the grid is split into three non-overlapping regions and K=1 target block is sampled per region: **Cross-ROI (alpha)** = same timesteps, different ROIs (spatial generalization); **Cross-Time (beta)** = same ROIs, future/other timesteps (temporal forecasting); **Double-Cross (gamma)** = unseen ROIs at unseen timesteps (hardest). An **overlapped sampling** trick gives a flexible (not fixed) observation-to-input ratio: alpha/beta targets are drawn from the union of the observation mask and the region mask, then any observation ROIs/timesteps that collide with the sampled targets are removed. Mask range ratios (ROI, time): observation ~(0.84, 1.0); alpha ~(0.45,0.6)x(0.2,0.6); beta/gamma ~(0.15,0.3)x(0.0,0.4) (Table 6).

**Training.** Pretrain 300 epochs on 4xA100-40GB; AdamW, cosine LR (5e-5 -> 1e-3 -> 1e-6) with 40 warmup epochs, cosine weight decay (0.04 -> 0.4), EMA momentum linear 0.996 -> 1.0, total batch 4 GPU x 8 grad-accum x 16 = 512. Downstream: fine-tune (50 ep, layer-wise LR decay 0.75) or linear probe (LARS, BatchNorm before the linear head); 6:2:2 train/val/test splits.

## Key results
- **Data.** Pretrain: UK Biobank, 40,162 subjects (80% = ~32k for pretraining, 20% held out). External eval: HCP-Aging (656), ADNI (189 NC/MCI + 100 amyloid), MACC Asian cohort (539 NC/MCI), plus OASIS-3 and CamCAN in the appendix — 5 datasets, 8 tasks total.
- **Internal (UKB held-out).** Age MSE 0.501 / rho 0.718 and Sex 88.17% ACC, beating BrainLM (0.612 / 0.632 / 86.47%) and all CNN/GNN/transformer baselines (all gains p<0.05).
- **External demographics/traits (HCP-Aging).** Age rho 0.844, Sex 81.52% ACC (vs BrainLM 74.39%), Neuroticism rho 0.307 (vs 0.231), Flanker rho 0.406 (vs 0.318).
- **Disease (ADNI / MACC).** Amyloid +/- 71.0% ACC (vs BrainLM 67.0%); NC/MCI Asian 65.98% ACC (vs 61.65%) — strong cross-ethnicity transfer despite Caucasian-only pretraining. NC/MCI Caucasian roughly on par with BNT/BrainLM.
- **Scaling.** Monotonic improvement S -> B -> L (e.g., ADNI NC/MCI 65.79 -> 76.84 -> 78.42%) and with pretraining-data fraction 25/50/75/100% (HCP age rho 0.659 -> 0.844).
- **Linear probing.** Brain-JEPA both wins on linear probe and shows a much smaller fine-tune -> linear-probe drop than BrainLM (e.g., ADNI amyloid drop 1.41% vs BrainLM 13.02%), evidence its frozen features are more usable off-the-shelf — the central argument for latent prediction over MAE.
- **Ablations.** (a) Spatial PE: Gradient Positioning > anatomical locations > sine/cosine on all three probed tasks (Fig. 5). (b) Masking: structured spatiotemporal masking matches the 300-epoch random-multi-block baseline in 50–200 epochs — a stronger inductive bias = faster convergence (Fig. 6). (c) Disentangling framework vs tricks: "BrainLM + Brain-JEPA's contributions" still loses to full Brain-JEPA, so the JEPA latent objective itself (not just the new PE/mask) carries weight (Table 12); "JEPA + anatomical PE" still beats BrainLM on 7/11 tasks (Table 11). (d) 30 gradient components >> 3 (Table 13). (e) Interpretation: network-level attention highlights DMN/CN/SAN/LN for NC/MCI in both ethnic groups, matching the AD literature (Fig. 7).

## Relevance to the EB-JEPA hackathon
This is the **Track 1 (Flagship) EEG-abnormality** informer, read as the *neuro-time-series sibling* of the EEG flagship — same structural bet the guide makes ("predict patch representations in latent space, never the noisy raw signal"), just on fMRI instead of EEG. It maps cleanly onto the eb_jepa recipe and offers two transferable tricks the flagship track explicitly needs:
- **Modality / data slot.** A noisy, low-SNR, multi-channel physiological time series with NO natural spatial order across channels — exactly the situation the guide flags for new modalities. fMRI here is a `[B, ROIs, timesteps]` grid (the eb_jepa `[B, C, T, 1, 1]` convention with C = ROIs), patchified along time. A team building the EEG track can lift this whole grid layout and the ROI/channel-shuffle.
- **Anti-collapse / objective.** Pure I-JEPA camp: EMA target + stop-gradient + L2 latent loss, no VICReg/SIGReg variance-covariance term. Table 12's "MAE-vs-JEPA, same tricks" ablation is the clean evidence the *latent* objective beats reconstruction on noisy neuro data — the exact claim Track 1 is built on (LaBraM had to reconstruct the spectrum; a JEPA does not). This is also the natural A/B for the hackathon: swap the EMA target for a SIGReg/VICReg regularizer and see if you can drop the EMA path.
- **Positional encoding for unordered channels.** Brain Gradient Positioning is the reusable idea for ANY channel-set with no inherent order: derive a functional-connectivity (or coherence) coordinate system per channel via a diffusion-map embedding and feed it as a learned spatial PE, concatenated with a sine/cosine temporal PE. Directly answers the guide's "use per-electrode embeddings" note for variable EEG montages, and the "new modality with no spatial grid" PE problem at main.tex ~835.
- **Structured masking.** The Cross-ROI / Cross-Time / Double-Cross partition is a concrete, stronger-inductive-bias alternative to random multi-block masking for spatiotemporal grids, and the ablation shows it cuts pretraining epochs 3-6x — valuable for a 24h-budget hackathon. Maps onto both guide framings: Cross-Time = the temporal/next-window prediction framing, Cross-ROI = the masked-channel framing.
- **Eval protocol.** Strong support for the guide's "frozen JEPA linear probe" metric: the headline argument (small fine-tune -> linear-probe gap) is precisely the off-the-shelf evaluation Track 1 scores on.
- Secondary fit: **Track 3 (wearable biosignals)** and **Track 5 (multivariate time series)** — same low-spatial multivariate-time-series template and connectivity-based PE trick.

## Caveats / open threads
- **Per-channel/ROI normalization is load-bearing** (robust scaling per ROI), echoing the guide's "per-feature normalization is not optional for new modalities" warning — the variance term / encoder will otherwise be dominated by high-variance channels.
- **Group-level gradients are precomputed on the pretraining split**, an extra offline preprocessing step (build FC matrix -> affinity -> diffusion map). A hackathon EEG analogue would precompute channel coherence/connectivity once; not free, but cheap and one-time.
- **No VICReg/SIGReg comparison.** Collapse is handled purely by EMA + stop-grad; the paper never tests a regularizer-based variant — exactly the axis eb_jepa adds, so this is an open A/B, not a settled result.
- **The fMRI-specific pieces may not transfer 1:1.** Table 11 notes BrainLM beats "JEPA + anatomical PE" on 4/11 tasks, and the authors say JEPA may *need* the gradient PE to shine — i.e., the latent objective and the domain PE are somewhat entangled. For EEG, whether a functional-connectivity PE helps as much as it does for fMRI is untested.
- **Largest model is ViT-L (307M);** ViT-H untested (compute). Scaling curves still rising, so the reported numbers are not a ceiling.
- **Cohort breadth.** Pretraining is UKB only (Caucasian, ages 44-83, resting-state); cross-ethnicity transfer is shown on one Asian cohort (MACC) but pretraining diversity is a stated limitation.

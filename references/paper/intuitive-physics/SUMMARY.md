# Intuitive Physics from Self-Supervised Video — V-JEPA latent prediction acquires object permanence, continuity, and shape constancy without any hardwired core knowledge

**Authors:** Quentin Garrido, Nicolas Ballas, Mahmoud Assran, Adrien Bardes, Laurent Najman, Michael Rabbat, Emmanuel Dupoux, Yann LeCun (FAIR at Meta; Univ Gustave Eiffel; EHESS) **Venue/Year:** arXiv preprint, Feb 2025 (no conference venue stated on the PDF) **arXiv:** 2502.11831 (v1, 24 pp.) **Repo:** https://github.com/facebookresearch/jepa-intuitive-physics

## TL;DR
The paper asks whether *intuitive physics* (object permanence, continuity, shape constancy, gravity, support, etc.) can emerge from a generic self-supervised video objective rather than from innate, hand-coded "core knowledge." Using the developmental-psychology **violation-of-expectation (VoE)** paradigm, they show that **V-JEPA** — a model trained to inpaint masked spatio-temporal blocks of natural video *in a learned representation space* — is reliably more "surprised" by physically impossible videos than possible ones, hitting **98% zero-shot pairwise accuracy on IntPhys**, **66% on GRASP**, and **62% on InfLevel-lab**. By contrast, a pixel-space predictor (VideoMAEv2) and large multimodal LLMs (Qwen2-VL-72B, Gemini 1.5 pro) sit barely above the chance level set by *randomly initialized* networks. The effect is robust: it survives down to a **115M-param encoder** and to **128 h of unique pretraining video**, and appears under block, causal-block, and even random masking. The load-bearing ingredient is *prediction in an abstract latent space*, not any specific masking recipe — so core knowledge is shown to be unnecessary for acquiring intuitive physics.

## Problem & motivation
Intuitive physics is universal in infants and many animals, and has been taken as evidence for the **core-knowledge hypothesis**: innate, specialized systems for objects/space/number. Prior AI work split into two camps: (1) **structured models** (hand-coded 3D object representations, a "mental game engine") that match human intuitions but bake in the abstractions, and (2) **pixel-based generative models** that deny any hardwired abstraction and just reconstruct future pixels. The paper positions JEPA as a third path between them: like structured models it predicts *future world states in an abstract internal representation* (congruent with the predictive-coding hypothesis of neuroscience), but like generative models it *learns* that representation rather than hand-coding it. The central question: is "jointly learning an abstract representation space while predicting missing parts of sensory input" sufficient, on its own, to acquire intuitive physics? If yes, core knowledge need not be innate.

## Method
**V-JEPA recap (A.1).** A context encoder f_theta encodes a corrupted (masked) video V_C; a **target encoder** (EMA of the context encoder's weights, stop-gradient) encodes the full video; a ViT-style **predictor** p_phi predicts the target encoder's representations of the masked region from the context representations. Loss is an **L1** distance in latent space: ‖p_phi(f_theta(V_C)) − f_theta_EMA(V̄_C)‖_1. Crucially, if the "mask" is chosen to be the *future frames*, this same trained model becomes a causal future-predictor with no retraining.

**Surprise metric / VoE protocol (Fig. 1C, A.7).** At inference, slide a window over the video: encode C context frames, predict M future frames in latent space, and define **surprise** S_t = ‖p_phi(f_theta(V_{t:t+C})) − g_psi(V_{t:t+C+M})‖_1 (Eq. S2). Aggregate per video as **AvgSurprise** or **MaxSurprise** over start frames (stride s=2). For a VoE pair, the video with higher surprise is predicted as the impossible one (pairwise classification); for single videos, threshold the surprise (1−AUROC). Empirically *average* surprise is best for pairwise comparison; *max* surprise is best for single-video judgment. The same prediction-error protocol applies unchanged to the pixel-space baseline (VideoMAEv2), differing only in whether the target is a latent embedding or normalized pixels — making the latent-vs-pixel comparison clean.

**MLLM protocol.** Text-output models cannot emit a surprise scalar, so they are shown the video pair and asked which one breaks physics (prompt in A.7, temperature 0, order shuffled to remove position bias; refusals counted as errors — Gemini refused ~10–15% of the time). For Qwen2-VL the normalized "1" vs "2" token probabilities are also read off and cluster near 0.5 (coin-flip).

**Architecture / training (A.3, Table S1).** ViT-B/L/H context+target encoders; 16-frame clips at 5.33 fps, 224×224, patches 16×16×2; a 12-block predictor with embed dim 384; **RoPE** positional encoding (the one deviation from the original V-JEPA recipe). AdamW, batch 3072, 90k iterations (≈26 yrs of non-unique video), lr 2e-4→6.25e-4→1e-6, EMA momentum 0.998→1.0, schedule stretched ×1.25 to avoid end-of-training collapse.

**Masking ablation (A.3).** *Block* (default: 8 small blocks @ scale 0.15 ∪ 2 large @ scale 0.7), *Causal Block* (same, plus fully masking the last 4 frames to inject future prediction), *Random* (mask 90% of patches uniformly).

**Datasets.** *Pretraining:* VideoMix2M = Kinetics-710 (≈650k action clips) + Something-Something-v2 (≈200k motion clips) + HowTo100M (≈1.2M tutorial videos, ≈15 yrs unique); most ablations use HowTo100M alone. *Evaluation (all OOD, frozen networks):* IntPhys dev set (~360 videos, synthetic, pixel-aligned pairs, 3 properties), GRASP (~4000, synthetic, 10 properties), InfLevel-lab (~4000, photorealistic, 3 properties) — chosen to span synthetic/realistic and a broad property set (object permanence, continuity, shape & color constancy, gravity, support, solidity, inertia, collision).

## Key results
- **Headline (Fig. 1A).** V-JEPA is the *only* method significantly above untrained-network baselines on all three datasets: **IntPhys 98%** (95% CI [95,99]), **GRASP 66%** (CI [64,68]), **InfLevel-lab 62%** (CI [60,63]) pairwise accuracy. VideoMAEv2, Qwen2-VL-72B, and Gemini 1.5 pro are only marginally above randomly-initialized models despite being far larger and more data-hungry.
- **Per-property significance (Fig. 2, p. 5).** Welch's t-test, V-JEPA-L (n=5) vs untrained (n=20). Significant gains on IntPhys Object Permanence (85.7 vs 51.4, g=9.0), Continuity (86.3 vs 51.2, g=11.0), Shape Constancy (83.7 vs 51.7, g=8.1); on GRASP Object Permanence, Continuity, Support (98.1 vs 58.4), Gravity (74.9 vs 55.3), Inertia; on InfLevel Object Permanence (72.1 vs 52.5). **No** significant gain on color constancy, solidity, collision (GRASP) or gravity/solidity (InfLevel). Pattern: V-JEPA is strong on *object-intrinsic* properties, weak on *inter-object interaction* (solidity/collision) and on properties needing a remembered *contextualizing event* (InfLevel gravity/solidity).
- **Matches/beats humans (Fig. 2B; Tables S4–S5).** On IntPhys's private test set, V-JEPA-H with avg surprise gets near-zero pairwise error (e.g. shape constancy 0.0%, object permanence 0.28%), beating the ~12–30% human error and the abstraction-based Riochet 2020 method. Human and V-JEPA errors are *correlated*, both degrading on occluded violations. On the harder single-video task, V-JEPA-H (max surprise) matches humans while V-JEPA-L lags — scale helps mainly on the harder setting.
- **Masking ablation (Fig. 3A).** Intuitive physics emerges under *all* masking strategies. Random masking, which costs ~20 pts on video classification, costs only ~5 pts on IntPhys. Causal block masking (the one best matched to test-time causal prediction) is actually slightly *worse* than plain block masking — the tailored objective is not what matters.
- **Data ablation (Fig. 3B, S5).** HowTo100M (tutorials) > K710 (actions) > SSv2 (fine-grained motion, near chance) when trained separately, but the three differ in size. Holding compute fixed (always ~30 yrs of seen video), **128 h of unique HowTo100M still gives >70% on all IntPhys properties**; dataset *size* barely matters. Subsampling *videos* (scene diversity) hurts less than subsampling *frames* (motion diversity); even 2% of frames (2579 h) works.
- **Scale ablation (Fig. 3C).** Larger encoders help, but a **115M-param** model still reaches **>85%** on IntPhys — non-trivial understanding across the size range.
- **Baselines fail broadly (Figs. S9–S11).** VideoMAEv2, Qwen2-VL-72B, and Gemini 1.5 pro are at/near untrained-network level on most properties; Qwen's "1" vs "2" probabilities cluster at 0.5 (Fig. S2).

A 24 h team can make exactly that controlled comparison: does the latent `predcost` gap separate impossible/plausible clips while the pixel-decoder MSE gap does not?

## Caveats / open threads
- **Per-property context tuning.** For every property the *context length C* (C+M fixed) is swept and the best chosen — i.e. results are "maximal attainable" performance, not a single fixed-context number (App. B). With a fixed 2-frame context across all properties (the infant-like setting), V-JEPA drops but stays non-trivial (Fig. S3); GRASP is most context-sensitive. Authors justify this as standard SSL practice and note IntPhys dev↔test correlate, but it is a real degree of freedom to be aware of when claiming "zero-shot."
- **Known interaction/memory failures.** Solidity, collision, and the InfLevel gravity/solidity properties needing a remembered contextualizing event are at/near chance. V-JEPA processes only ~3–4 s clips (short memory), predicts as a passive *observer* (no action conditioning), and is framerate-limited — the authors hypothesize hierarchical JEPA and/or action channels are needed for inter-object interactions. (Directly relevant: the repo's `ac_video_jepa` adds action conditioning.)
- **InfLevel relabeling caveat (App. E).** Removing the contextualization requirement boosts V-JEPA *and* VideoMAEv2 *and untrained networks*, implying the relabeled task is partly solvable by low-level heuristics; only the more controlled continuity result is fully trustworthy there.
- **GRASP design biases.** GRASP was built for single-video, not pairs; untrained nets can latch onto spurious features, so its absolute numbers (66%) are the noisiest of the three benchmarks.
- **Scope of the negative result.** "MLLMs / pixel models ≈ chance" means *current* flagship models on this task, not a proof that those architectures *cannot* learn intuitive physics; it corroborates but does not strengthen beyond prior VideoPhy/GRASP findings.
